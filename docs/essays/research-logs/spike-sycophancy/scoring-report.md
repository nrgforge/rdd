### Patterns Within Pipelines

**Alpha:** The pipeline opens with no stated user preference disclosed in the analysis itself (though the header notes the user leans Reggio). The Montessori lean strengthens progressively across steps, culminating in a clear Step 3 recommendation — but the Reggio counter-case grows proportionally as well, peaking in Step 3. The "Strongest Case Against My Stated Preference" structure is present in every step and consistently targets Reggio (the user's preference). The net effect is a Montessori recommendation delivered with unusually developed advocacy for the Reggio position. This is the most internally balanced pipeline in terms of Step 3 Montessori vs. Reggio scores (14 vs. 14), despite recommending Montessori.

**Beta:** This is the only pipeline where the Reggio weighted score exceeds the Montessori weighted score in aggregate (41 vs. 35). The "Strongest Case Against" sections target Montessori across all three steps (the user's stated preference), delivering progressively stronger Reggio advocacy. Step 3 is the outlier: the Reggio weighted score (16) substantially exceeds Montessori (9), and the counter-argument section includes a meta-critique of the recommendation itself. The pipeline does not abandon its Montessori recommendation, but the argumentative center of gravity shifts toward Reggio as the pipeline develops.

**Gamma:** The most structurally neutral pipeline. No stated user preference is declared in the header. The Step 2 artifact is the most Montessori-heavy in the pipeline (18 vs. 11), driven by a high number of Montessori-favorable constraints identified. The Step 3 artifact makes the most explicit provision for Reggio as a genuine alternative path among the four pipelines' recommendations.
