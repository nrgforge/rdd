## Interpretation

### Pattern

The signals do not converge on a narrowing pattern. They converge on the opposite: the DISCOVER phase's most consequential moments were the two points where the solution space widened — the Section 1 reframe and the gate's bolt-on challenge — and both were recorded in the artifact. This contrasts sharply with RESEARCH Moments 1–4, where the narrowing was the pattern.

The clearest residual risk is a register-level issue rather than a framing-adoption issue in the sycophancy-gradient sense. The Zero-Prior-Familiarity Reader's stakeholder description cites methodology literature (Sweller, Pirolli & Card, Kalyuga) by name in the product-discovery document's body. This is the canonical failure mode the dispatch prompt names: "research-essay framings inherited into product-facing language without being tested against user voice." The citation pattern makes a stakeholder description read like an essay footnote rather than a user-voice rendering of a real user's experience. A non-author reader of product-discovery.md encounters three cognitive-science proper nouns before they encounter what this stakeholder says, wants, or does.

The second signal worth separating is the spectrum-to-binary collapse question. Five value tensions were evaluated:

- **T1 (initial-handoff vs. ongoing-reference):** Recorded with an explicit hold-open: *"Held: how much can content-layering compensate before the reading-task mismatch forces splitting into separate artifacts?"* The tension does not collapse the spectrum. The "threading is wrong in principle" alternative (Nguyen et al. 2025) is separately encoded in Assumption Inversion A3 as not adopted but live. No collapse.

- **T2 (outcome test vs. reading-cost test):** This is where the most consequential gate work happened. The gate's belief-mapping question caught the binary framing and the user's response surfaced a third frame — bolt-on / felt-additional as a failure-mode signal encoding an in-place-first operating principle. The artifact now records: (a) the binary as value tension, (b) the third frame in the stakeholder section revision, (c) the bolt-on / felt-additional vocabulary in Product Vocabulary. The third frame was genuinely surfaced and committed. No unexamined collapse.

- **T3 (cognitive vs. non-cognitive admissibility):** Recorded as explicitly held for DECIDE. The gate reflection note names this as an open question. No collapse.

- **T4 (multi-item agent turn structure — bundling vs. upstream decomposition):** Recorded as held with a specific unresolved question: where does decomposition happen structurally so it fires under task load? No collapse.

- **T5 (AI-as-orienter vs. AI-as-prosthetic):** Recorded as held with the specific uncertainty noted: no tested operational criterion distinguishing the two in practice. Adoption of agent-mediated orientation blocked on this. No collapse.

The field-notes correction episode — the agent proposed a field-notes-derived annotation from a 2026-03-27 play session that predates Cycle 017, which the user corrected — does not reveal a broader pattern. The session-task record shows no other instances of cross-session source extraction. The correction was specific and immediately accepted; the incident is isolated.

### Earned Confidence vs. Sycophantic Reinforcement

The phase shows characteristics of earned confidence:

1. The gate's belief-mapping question produced a genuine third frame the artifact had not encoded, and the practitioner's response was substantively different from either binary the question offered.
2. The resulting artifact revision is not additive decoration — it changes the operating principle encoded in Section 1 from "admit brief on-ramps when outcome test passes" to "in-place-first as default; bolt-on artifacts that feel additional are a failure-mode signal."
3. The practitioner's response also caught a second agent misjudgment (the §4.1 finding on audience-based directory separation conflated with infrastructure-vs-product separation), correcting the agent's proposed solution scoping. This is adversarial engagement, not passive acceptance.
4. The propose-and-confirm pattern in sections 2 through 6 produced brief accepts at the section level, but the accepts followed proposals that correctly tracked the cycle's established direction — the user had already, through the Section 1 reframe and the gate's third-frame surfacing, demonstrated where consequential decisions lived. Brief accepts elsewhere are appropriate given that evidence.

The register-level signal — methodology voice inside user-voice sections — is a writing-register issue, not a sycophancy-gradient narrowing. The distinction matters because the remediation is different: a Grounding Reframe targets a framing decision that downstream phases will inherit as a settled premise. The register issue targets a readability convention that downstream phases will not inherit as a premise but may re-encounter as a vocabulary pattern in the model phase.

The rest of the product-discovery document does not carry parenthetical academic citations in its stakeholder descriptions. This stakeholder is the only one that cites its sources inline.

This meets the criteria for a feed-forward signal but not for a Grounding Reframe — the model phase does not inherit this as a framing premise that narrows the solution space; it inherits it as a register note that can be corrected when the stakeholder's needs are encoded in domain-model vocabulary.

### Reasoning

The RESEARCH snapshot's Target 1 was the cycle's primary sycophancy risk at the discover → model boundary. That target has been discharged at this gate: the practitioner's bolt-on / felt-additional response produced a third frame that sharpens the artifact's position beyond either binary the gate's belief-mapping question offered. The discharge is genuine: the artifact records a sharper position with the alternative examined, not the user's position without examination of the alternative.

The five value tensions T1–T5 do not show unexamined binary collapse. T1, T3, T4, and T5 are held with explicit uncertainty markers. T2's third frame was surfaced at the gate and committed. The "threading is wrong in principle" alternative persists as Assumption Inversion A3 without adoption — this is the correct disposition for a live alternative that the cycle's resolution does not foreclose.

The field-notes correction is isolated and does not reveal a pattern.

### Feed-forward signal for the model phase

The Zero-Prior-Familiarity Reader stakeholder description (Section 1) carries parenthetical academic citations (Sweller; Pirolli & Card; Kalyuga's expertise reversal effect) in a section whose register is user voice, not methodology voice. The model phase imports this stakeholder into the domain model and the gate reflection note explicitly assigns the domain-glossary Product Origin column as the artifact where provenance belongs. When the model phase encodes the Zero-Prior-Familiarity Reader's needs in domain vocabulary, the encoding should translate the methodology-voice citation into user-voice expression of what this stakeholder does, wants, and cannot do without. The parenthetical citations belong in the Product Origin column, not in the stakeholder description itself. This is a writing-register correction the model phase is positioned to apply during its import step without reopening any substantive design question.

No other feed-forward signals are warranted. The discover → model boundary is clean on the framing-adoption and spectrum-collapse dimensions the dispatch evaluation was asked to assess.
