# ADR-092: Essay-Outline as the RESEARCH Artifact Form

**Status:** Accepted (Cycle 018 BUILD shipped WP-A; v0.8.6)

## Context

The `/rdd-research` skill currently produces a written prose essay as its primary artifact, audited by citation audit, argument audit, and framing audit before the gate. Cycle 018 investigated two convergent questions: whether the prose essay is the right form for the artifact RESEARCH produces (the user's everyday observation that AI-generated research essays are not human-read), and how RDD's phases compare against the CRESS principles for context engineering (Codemanship, 2026-05-04). The two threads were treated as potentially co-illuminating rather than independent, and the cycle's research artifacts (`./docs/essays/017-outlines-as-research-artifacts-prose.md` and `./docs/essays/017-outlines-as-research-artifacts-outline.md` — produced via Dogfooded Both-Form Production) converged on a form-change proposal.

The form change is justified on three driver-derived grounds. The trio aligns with the prose-form essay's §7 "central reframing" articulation — the cycle's affirmative case for outline form (rather than the comparative case against the strongest rejected alternative, which Loop C bears in Rejected Alternative #4). This is the trio examined and confirmed at the DECIDE gate; an earlier framing in the post-research feed-forward synthesis substituted Loop C for the downstream-consumption ground, and that substitution was re-examined and reverted at the DECIDE gate against the empirical anchor of the R2 corpus check:

- **Susceptibility-Surface Area minimization.** Prose form has more surface for framing-adoption susceptibility than outline form — connective tissue between explicit claims is where invisible synthesis can hide. The reduction is theoretically motivated (Loop B/C reasoning) and empirically directional within Cycle 018 (citation-error asymmetry plus the prose-auditor's Self-Referential Meta-Observation), not statistically established.
- **CRESS-Empirical compatibility.** The outline form aligns with CRESS's empirically-grounded, retrieval-anchored, structurally-explicit principles for context engineering (the convergence treated as via analogy per the Target 2 caveat below).
- **Downstream-AI-consumption preservation.** RESEARCH artifacts feed downstream phases (DISCOVER, MODEL, DECIDE, ARCHITECT) as agent-consumed context. The R2 corpus-consumption analysis in Cycle 018 grep'd this codebase's downstream artifacts and identified three distinct consumption modes: bibliographic provenance (12 references in `roadmap.md` + 4 in `ORIENTATION.md` + 2 in `field-guide.md`), section-keyed warrants (used throughout `system-design.agents.md` as the architectural-driver pattern), and substantive content reuse (96 essay references across ADRs, plus product-discovery and system-design references). What downstream phases consume substantively is structured content — named claims, framework introductions, synthesis statements — not prose flow. Outline form preserves these consumption units explicitly via the Argument-Graph plus Outline-Production Discipline's synthesis-bullet requirement (§5); bare claim-listing would degrade downstream consumption. Scope qualifications: (a) the R2 analysis is scoped to this codebase's corpus (16 essays + this codebase's downstream artifacts); (b) the `grep`-based explicit-citation method captures a floor on substantive consumption, not a ceiling (implicit content reuse where downstream artifacts adopt a framework concept without citing the essay by number is not captured); (c) the analysis measures how the existing *prose-essay* corpus is consumed — it characterizes the consumption units the form must preserve, but it does not measure predicted outline-essay consumption. The form-change's affirmative case rests on the inference that the consumption-unit set (claims, framework introductions, synthesis statements) is form-independent and that outline form preserves them explicitly via the Argument-Graph + Outline-Production Discipline conditionality. Future cycles producing Essay-Outlines will accumulate the parallel evidence that closes the inference; until then, the affirmative case is grounded in unit-set observation plus a structural-preservation claim, not in a multi-cycle outline-vs-prose consumption comparison. The empirical anchor is direct (observed consumption modes; counted references) and bears on the form change's primary affirmative case: the form must preserve what downstream consumes, and outline form does, conditional on the synthesis-bullet discipline.

A fourth consideration — the **Loop C critical-flaw argument** — bears the structural load of the comparative case against the strongest rejected alternative (audit-extended prose with mandated CALIBRATION bullets): audit-extended prose cannot reliably separate rhetorical force from logical force in AI-agent-produced text. Loop C is deployed in Rejected Alternative #4 below, where its comparative-case role does its strongest argumentative work. It is not a co-equal load-bearing justification in the affirmative trio above.

Cycle 018's DISCOVER phase shifted the burden of proof to narrative form: the user found prose form does not propagate information forward in the RESEARCH artifact role; narrative remains useful where the goal is *sharing* (the SYNTHESIZE phase, unchanged). The form change is RESEARCH-scoped; the existing SYNTHESIZE pattern (agent produces outline; user writes shareable prose from it) is the architectural precedent the form change extends upstream to RESEARCH.

At the MODEL gate the user introduced two structural commitments that became domain concepts in Amendment 23: **Pyramid Refinement** as Essay-Outline's structural property (the four sections form a refinement hierarchy in which each level expands cleanly into the next), and **Outline-Coherence Signal** as the stewardship trigger that fires when the pyramid cannot read cleanly within a cycle's scope. The MODEL-gate Susceptibility Snapshot flagged that both concepts were adopted in the same exchange that received them; three Grounding Reframe targets were pursued at the gate:

- **Target 1 (grounded):** Pyramid Refinement was retro-evaluated against the existing Cycle 018 outline-form essay; operational compliance and violation tests were derived (see §3 below).
- **Target 2 (grounded — scope expansion):** the existing `argument-auditor.md` agent definition specifies five within-document verifications plus framing audit, but does NOT specify Argument-Graph consumption, graph-traversal, or expansion-fidelity verification. The user's option (a) commitment is correct in scope but per Invariant 8 the mechanism is judgment-anchored as-is. A separate ADR (ADR-093) files the Skill-Structure Layer amendment to `argument-auditor.md` so that this ADR's auditor-integration claim is anchored.
- **Target 3 (grounded):** Outline-Coherence Signal's discrimination test was added — abstract → argument-graph expansion failure routes to scope; argument-graph → citation-embedded outline expansion failure routes to discipline.

Two scope-of-claim caveats inherited from earlier in the cycle apply to any claim in this ADR that cites the convergence or the citation-comparison evidence:

- **Target 2 caveat (CRESS scope-transfer via analogy).** The CRESS-convergence argument rests on the analogy "CRESS describes properties of context fed to LLM agents; RDD phases produce artifacts that function as context fed to downstream LLM agents." The two-thread convergence (practice + CRESS audit) is supportive but reaches the same diagnostic through a shared analogy rather than through two fully independent frameworks.
- **Target 3 caveat (citation-comparison evidence scoped to one prose-production method).** The 3 unique-to-prose P1 citation errors vs. 0 unique-to-outline P1 errors is scoped to Cycle 018's prose-production method (synthesis from research log with re-attribution at synthesis time). A future cycle producing prose directly from primary sources would warrant re-running the comparison before treating the citation-error advantage as settled.

The two open questions DECIDE must close are inherited from MODEL feed-forward: (i) synthesis-bullet discipline anchoring — structural (audit-time verification) or conventional (skill-text instruction); (ii) Argument-Graph format specification — text-based dependency graph, mermaid, or structured-bullet hierarchy with named identifiers. The form-migration scope for reflections and the SYNTHESIZE synthesis essay is held separable (the form change is RESEARCH-scoped).

## Decision

The methodology adopts **Essay-Outline** as the canonical RESEARCH artifact form for `/rdd-research`. The form is RESEARCH-scoped; SYNTHESIZE's existing outline-then-user-writes-prose pattern is unchanged.

### 1. The four-section structure

An Essay-Outline contains exactly four sections, in order:

1. **Abstract Section.** Succinctly presents the research and the conclusions. Provides a shareable surface without restoring prose body. Each abstract claim must map cleanly to one or more Argument-Graph nodes below.
2. **Argument-Graph Section.** The claim-warrant-evidence dependency structure as first-class structural content. Distinct from the argument-auditor's report (which produces findings *about* the graph); the section IS the graph. Elevates argument structure from auditor-output to deliverable content. Format specified in §4.
3. **Citation-Embedded Outline.** The body — hierarchical bullets with inline citations at claim level. Each Argument-Graph node expands into a body subsection whose bullets carry the working that grounds it. Outline-Production Discipline (§5) governs production.
4. **References.** Bibliography for citations used throughout. Citation entries match the inline citations in the Citation-Embedded Outline.

File naming pattern: `essay-outline-NNN-<slug>.md` under `./docs/essays/`. The numbering continues the existing essay sequence; the filename pattern marks the form change. Existing prose essays remain at their existing paths under their existing names — the form change applies to RESEARCH artifacts produced after this ADR is accepted; no retroactive migration.

### 2. Pyramid Refinement as the structural property

The four sections form a refinement hierarchy, not peer sections. "Expand cleanly" is load-bearing:

- The Abstract expands cleanly into the Argument-Graph (the abstract's claims decompose into argument-graph nodes).
- The Argument-Graph expands cleanly into the Citation-Embedded Outline (each graph node expands into the body's working bullets and citations that ground it).
- The Citation-Embedded Outline traces to the References (every citation in the outline appears in the references; every reference is cited somewhere).

Each level is information-preserving and information-extending; the next level shows the working that the prior level summarizes. The audit's job is to verify the pyramid holds (level-to-level expansion fidelity), not just within-level argument validity.

**Three structural alternatives to the refinement hierarchy were considered and not adopted** (named by the MODEL-gate susceptibility snapshot as the warrant-gap targets and addressed at the gate via Target 1 grounding):

- **(a) Flat four equal sections** — Abstract, Argument-Graph, Citation-Embedded Outline, and References as independently valid sections, not necessarily related by refinement. Rejected because the sections are not informationally symmetric: the abstract synthesizes what the outline develops; treating them as peers would sever the expansion-fidelity verification target that licenses the Outline-Coherence Signal stewardship trigger (§6) and that ADR-093's argument-audit relies on. The compliance test (a)–(d) in §3 cannot be defined against flat peer sections; it requires the level boundaries that the hierarchy creates.
- **(b) Two-level hierarchy** — Abstract (summary) → everything else (structured content). Rejected because the Argument-Graph → Citation-Embedded Outline expansion is itself a refinement relationship (graph nodes expand into body working) that disappears under a two-level structure. Losing the graph-to-outline traversal is exactly the auditor-scope gap ADR-093 §2 is designed to anchor; collapsing the levels would require ADR-093 to verify a relationship the structure does not encode. Three levels is the minimum that preserves the auditor's traversal across each named refinement.
- **(c) Conventional rather than structural framing** — the pyramid as a recommended reading order or production order rather than as a structural property. Rejected because the compliance test (a)–(d) cannot license the Outline-Coherence Signal stewardship trigger without being structural: a "recommended ordering" cannot fail; only a "structural property" can fail in a way that signals scope or discipline problems. The framing's load-bearing role is to support stewardship; weakening it to convention removes the stewardship anchor.

These alternatives were named by the MODEL gate's susceptibility snapshot (Finding 1) as warrant-gap candidates that had not been engaged before Pyramid Refinement was adopted as a domain concept. The Target 1 Grounding Reframe at the same gate confirmed that "expand cleanly" is operationalizable on a worked example (the existing Cycle 018 outline-form essay); it did not itself perform the three-level-vs-flat / two-level / conventional comparison. The rejection reasoning above is DECIDE-phase synthesis responsive to the snapshot's named gap — recorded here so a downstream reader can trace the rationale to its source (snapshot named the gap; DECIDE composed the rejections).

### 3. Pyramid Refinement compliance test (operational)

The compliance test is the structural definition of "the pyramid holds." Operational compliance requires:

- **(a) No orphan abstract claims.** Every conclusion in the Abstract has body content developing it (traceable through Argument-Graph nodes to Citation-Embedded Outline bullets).
- **(b) No orphan body sections.** Every section in the Citation-Embedded Outline cites evidence in the References.
- **(c) CLAIM / WARRANT / EVIDENCE bullets present within body sections.** Outline-Production Discipline visible in the body.
- **(d) With the explicit Argument-Graph section present:** every Argument-Graph node has an abstract-claim parent above and citation-embedded-outline children below; no orphan graph nodes in either direction.

Operational violation: any of (a)/(b)/(c)/(d) failing.

The compliance test was grounded against the existing Cycle 018 outline-form essay at the MODEL gate (Target 1 grounding action, 2026-05-14). The retro-evaluation confirmed operationalizability: each of the abstract's 5 CONCLUSIONS (C1–C5) traces to body sections developing it; body sections carry explicit CLAIM / WARRANT / EVIDENCE / CALIBRATION / SCOPE QUALIFICATION bullets.

### 4. Argument-Graph format — structured-bullet hierarchy with named identifiers

The Argument-Graph section uses a **structured-bullet hierarchy with named identifiers** as its format. Each node is identified by a hierarchical label and references its parent and source(s):

```markdown
- **C1.** [Claim text]
  - W1.1. [Warrant — the reasoning step that makes the evidence support the claim]
    - E1.1.1. [Evidence — citation reference, e.g., (Author 2026, p.N)]
    - E1.1.2. [Evidence — citation reference]
  - W1.2. [Warrant]
    - E1.2.1. [Evidence — citation reference]
- **C2.** [Claim text]
  - ...
```

Conventions:

- **Claims** (`C1`, `C2`, ...) at the top level correspond 1:1 with Abstract Section conclusions. The Abstract's conclusions are the claims; the Argument-Graph names them with stable identifiers.
- **Warrants** (`W1.1`, `W1.2`, ...) decompose how the evidence supports the claim. A claim may have multiple warrants (multiple lines of support).
- **Evidence** (`E1.1.1`, ...) cites references by reference key; the reference key resolves in the References section.
- **Citation-Embedded Outline** body subsections MUST anchor to one or more Argument-Graph node identifiers via a parenthetical at the end of the section heading (e.g., `### Section 3: Argument-graph parallels in research-essay corpora (C1)` or `### Section 7: Susceptibility evidence (W1.2, E1.2.1)`). The anchor enables the auditor to verify "this graph node expands cleanly into these outline bullets." Body content that genuinely does not develop a named graph node — for example, methodology preamble, scope-setting introductions, or appendix-style background — is anchored to a reserved identifier `META` (e.g., `### Methodology preamble (META)`) so the auditor recognizes the section as deliberately non-developmental rather than as a missing anchor. **META-anchored sections are subject to audit-time review:** a META section that contains claim / warrant / evidence bullets (i.e., that develops a graph node despite the META anchor) is reported by the argument-auditor as a P2 misclassification — the META anchor is a deliberate non-developmental marker, not an opt-out for developmental content under task load. This guards against the Invariant 8 risk that an author-declared exemption silently bypasses the structural check the MUST is designed to enforce.

Rejected formats (see Rejected Alternatives §1).

### 5. Synthesis-bullet discipline as Outline-Production Discipline (conventional anchoring) verified by Pyramid Refinement audit (structural verification of expansion)

Outline-Production Discipline — explicit Synthesis Bullets, scope-qualification bullets, CONFIDENCE-LEVEL tags, and named meta-moves (PROVENANCE CORRECTION, SCOPE QUALIFICATION) — is anchored **conventionally** in the `/rdd-research` skill text as production requirements for the Citation-Embedded Outline body. **Structural verification operates at the pyramid-expansion level, not at the per-bullet level.** The argument-audit (per ADR-093) verifies that:

- Each Argument-Graph node has body content developing it (the pyramid expansion holds; orphan graph nodes are flagged).
- The body content satisfies the compliance test (a)–(d) above.

The argument-audit does NOT enforce per-section presence of a specifically-labeled "SYNTHESIS" bullet. Synthesis content is verified by the pyramid expansion check: if an Argument-Graph node has body content that develops its warrants and evidence, the synthesis the node represents is present and audited; if the body content is absent or incoherent, the pyramid fails and the Outline-Coherence Signal fires per §6.

This decision separates the layers cleanly: discipline operates per-bullet in the body (skill-text convention); structural verification operates at the level boundaries (audit). The methodology does not assert that the convention layer alone is reliable under task load — Outline-Production Discipline's reliability under task load is an open question structurally analogous to the Cycle 10 "prose-hardening-doesn't-work" finding (disciplinary instructions in skill text failed under task load) that future cycles may revisit. What is anchored is the pyramid-expansion check, which fires structurally regardless of whether per-bullet discipline was achieved.

This claim depends on the argument-audit actually firing on Essay-Outline artifacts; the Skill-Structure Layer amendment in ADR-093 (Argument-Graph consumption + pyramid graph-traversal + expansion-fidelity verification) is the precondition that makes the structural catch reliable. Without ADR-093 implemented, the convention layer is the only backstop and the layer separation degrades to discipline-alone — the very Invariant 8 risk the form change is designed to mitigate. The two ADRs ship together; the layer separation's reliability is conditional on both decisions being implemented.

### 6. Outline-Coherence Signal as stewardship trigger with discrimination

When an Essay-Outline's pyramid does not read cleanly within a single cycle's scope, the form has structurally exposed a problem. The discrimination test (MODEL gate Target 3 grounding action, 2026-05-14) routes the diagnosis:

- **Abstract → Argument-Graph expansion fails** (conclusions cannot be decomposed into a coherent dependency graph; conclusions are too synthetic to be a single decomposable argument). → **Scope is suspect.** The cycle is researching more than one cohesive argument can carry. Stewardship response: split the cycle into multiple smaller cycles, each with a focused argument-graph.
- **Argument-Graph → Citation-Embedded Outline expansion fails** (the graph has nodes but the working bullets aren't there, or claims exist without citations). → **Discipline is suspect.** The cycle has the argument structure but the production work wasn't completed. Stewardship response: re-run the production with discipline tightening, no scope change required.
- **Abstract orphans** (abstract claims with no graph node or body section). → **Trace which level failed** to route the diagnosis; the absence-pattern alone doesn't discriminate.

The signal is a stewardship trigger, not an auto-action. The practitioner decides whether to split the cycle, tighten discipline, or accept the artifact with the gap recorded as a scope-of-claim caveat.

### 7. Scope-of-claim caveats encoded for downstream consumption

Any downstream artifact (ADRs, system design, research log, reflections) that cites the cycle's convergence between practice and CRESS, or that cites the citation-error comparison as motivation for the form change, must encode:

- **CRESS scope-transfer caveat.** The convergence is via analogy ("CRESS describes properties of context fed to LLM agents; RDD phases produce artifacts that function as context fed to downstream LLM agents"), not via two fully independent frameworks reaching the same diagnostic.
- **Citation-comparison scope caveat.** The 3 unique-to-prose vs. 0 unique-to-outline P1 errors is scoped to Cycle 018's prose-production method (synthesis from research log with re-attribution at synthesis time). A future cycle producing prose directly from primary sources warrants re-running the comparison.

### 8. Form-migration scope held separable

The form change applies to `/rdd-research` only. Held separable:

- **Reflections** (`./docs/essays/reflections/*.md`) — narrative form retained; reflections serve meaning-making and personal voice, not information propagation.
- **Synthesis Essay** (`/rdd-synthesize` output, when the user writes it) — narrative form retained; the SYNTHESIZE phase's existing pattern (agent produces outline; user writes prose from it) is unchanged.
- **ADRs, ORIENTATION, system-design, product-discovery, domain-model** — all retain their existing forms (none of these are essays in the `/rdd-research` sense).

Future cycles may revisit form-migration scope for reflections or other narrative artifacts; this ADR does not pre-decide those questions.

### 9. SYNTHESIZE precedent: this is the upstream extension

The form change is **not novel architecture** — it extends the existing `/rdd-synthesize` pattern (agent produces outline; user writes shareable prose from it) one phase upstream to `/rdd-research`. SYNTHESIZE already establishes outline-as-research-artifact + user-written-prose-as-sharing-act as a legitimate division of labor. The form change names what SYNTHESIZE already does and applies it where the user has identified an information-propagation-vs-sharing mismatch.

## Rejected alternatives

**1. Argument-Graph format: mermaid diagram.** Considered. Mermaid would provide visual rendering of dependency structure (a graphical advantage for human reading). Rejected for three reasons. First, mermaid requires a rendering dependency for the audit to consume it — the argument-auditor would need either a markdown-aware parser or to read mermaid syntax as text; the structured-bullet form is plain markdown the auditor reads natively. Second, mermaid's authorability at outline-production time is lower; the structured-bullet form composes during research-log synthesis without requiring the author to mentally render a graph. Third, mermaid's hierarchy is less linear than structured bullets — citation-embedded outline body subsections would not have a natural ordering to anchor against. The structured-bullet form supports the auditor's traversal needs and is the simpler format.

**2. Argument-Graph format: pure dependency graph (parser-required, e.g., a graph DSL).** Considered. A dedicated graph format would express dependency structure most precisely (multiple parents, cycles flagged structurally, etc.). Rejected because it would require parsing infrastructure the methodology does not currently have, and the precision gain over structured-bullet hierarchy is marginal for the cycle-rhythm scale at which RDD operates. The structured-bullet form admits multiple parents via reference (`E2.1.1. See also W1.2 — same evidence supports C2's W2.1`) when needed.

**3. Synthesis-bullet discipline anchored structurally per-section (audit-time SYNTHESIS-bullet-presence check).** Considered seriously — this is the Invariant 8 question the MODEL belief-mapping raised. Rejected in this form because the four-section structure with explicit Argument-Graph + Pyramid Refinement audit already operates at the *expansion* level, where synthesis content lives. Asserting "every body section must have a literal SYNTHESIS bullet" would conflate structural verification of expansion with discipline-level instruction about bullet labels. The chosen separation (Pyramid Refinement audit = structural; Outline-Production Discipline = convention) is honest about which layer each anchor operates at. The Invariant 8 risk that Outline-Production Discipline alone may fail under task load is a real cost; the methodology's response is to make the cost visible (Outline-Coherence Signal discriminating discipline-failure from scope-failure when the pyramid fails) rather than to claim the cost away via a structural check at the wrong level.

**4. Keep prose form; address susceptibility through audit-extended prose with mandated CALIBRATION bullets.** Considered as the conservative path. Rejected on the Loop C critical-flaw evidence — audit-extended prose does not reliably separate rhetorical force from logical force in AI-agent-produced text, so framing adoption can occur within a well-headed prose section without detection. CALIBRATION-bullet discipline within prose is structurally analogous to Outline-Production Discipline within outline, but the prose form retains the connective-tissue susceptibility surface that outline form removes. The Loop C argument is the load-bearing distinction with one explicit scope-of-claim caveat: the inference combines the Docherty & Smith observation (within-prose discipline insufficient against the connective-tissue surface) with an RDD-context inference about AI-agent-produced prose; the combined inference is the cycle's own synthesis rather than a peer-reviewed empirical finding for AI-agent-produced prose specifically. Future cycles producing multi-cycle audit comparisons between outline and audit-extended prose would either strengthen or refute the inference. The form change ships with that scope-of-claim caveat encoded; the rejection is operationally adequate at directional-plus-theoretical evidence and is open to revision if empirical evidence reverses the direction.

**5. Move to Cochrane hybrid (structured-abstract-plus-synthesis at full Cochrane-review scale).** Considered. Cochrane hybrid scores better than Essay-Outline on all three Loop B/C criteria (audit-fidelity, susceptibility-surface-minimization, downstream-feed-forward) — Target 1 grounding established this. Rejected on the scale-incompatibility caveat: Cochrane hybrid operates at full systematic-review scale (months of investigator effort, formal protocol development, structured search per inclusion criterion). RDD operates at cycle-rhythm scale (days to weeks per phase). Essay-Outline is the **Available Instantiation** of the structured-abstract-plus-synthesis family at RDD's scale — not "uniquely best by criterion comparison" but "the form within reach that satisfies susceptibility-surface-minimization without Cochrane-scale infrastructure cost."

**6. Form-migrate SYNTHESIZE alongside RESEARCH for symmetry.** Considered. The asymmetry between RESEARCH (form-changed) and SYNTHESIZE (unchanged) could feel unprincipled. Rejected because the cycle's framing names the distinction: narrative form is useful where the goal is *sharing* (SYNTHESIZE); narrative is not useful where the goal is *information propagation forward* (RESEARCH). The phases differ in role, so they appropriately differ in form. Symmetry-for-its-own-sake would override the role-difference.

**7. Defer the form change until a fully empirical test (multi-cycle production study) can establish susceptibility-surface-minimization statistically.** Considered. The Cycle 018 evidence is directional (citation-error asymmetry; prose-auditor self-referential meta-observation), not statistical. Rejected because the form change carries no operational regret cost — Essay-Outline is functionally a sub-case of structured-abstract-plus-synthesis with a clear migration path back to prose if a future cycle's evidence reverses the direction. Deferring would leave the methodology with an artifact form whose susceptibility-surface area has been examined and not addressed. The methodology amends the form, encodes the scope-of-claim caveats honestly, and future cycles may revisit if evidence accumulates against the direction.

**8. Reject the form change entirely; treat the prose form as canonically correct given the cycle's directional-only evidence.** Considered as the inversion. Rejected on three grounds: (i) the Loop C critical-flaw argument for outline over within-prose discipline is theoretical-plus-directional rather than multi-cycle empirical — the inference is the cycle's own synthesis combining the Docherty & Smith observation with an RDD-context AI-agent qualifier — but the structural mechanism (connective-tissue surface in prose hides framing-adoption events that outline form's claim-warrant-evidence visibility surfaces) is independent of statistical replication and remains the operative argument until either empirical reversal or a clearer counter-mechanism is identified; (ii) the user's burden-of-proof shift at DISCOVER (evidence the narrative form is useful in the RESEARCH role) is itself the cycle's substantive finding — the conservative-by-default direction has been re-examined and not defended; (iii) the form change extends an existing SYNTHESIZE pattern rather than introducing new architecture, so the operational risk surface is small.

## Consequences

**Positive:**

- The RESEARCH artifact form aligns with the role the artifact serves (information propagation forward, not sharing). The artifact form change names what was already true.
- The four-section structure with explicit Argument-Graph elevates argument structure from auditor-output to deliverable content, addressing the synthesis-bullet-reliability concern at the structural level (the graph IS the argument scaffolding; outline bullets expand it).
- The Pyramid Refinement compliance test gives the audit (per ADR-093) an operational target instead of an aspirational structural property.
- The Outline-Coherence Signal discriminates scope failures from discipline failures, so practitioners have an operational stewardship signal rather than a single ambiguous "the outline feels wrong" inference.
- Susceptibility-Surface Area is reduced for the artifact RESEARCH produces — the connective-tissue surface where framing-adoption can hide is removed in the outline form.
- The honest scope-of-claim (Available Instantiation rather than "uniquely best") preserves the methodology's epistemic integrity. Future cycles producing stronger evidence (statistical multi-cycle production studies; alternative prose-production methods) can update the framing without invalidating the form change.

**Negative:**

- Existing essay-form artifacts (`./docs/essays/*-NNN-*.md` produced before this ADR) remain in their existing form; the corpus mixes forms. This is the cost of "no retroactive migration." Future readers of the essay corpus encounter both forms and must orient to which form they're reading. Mitigation: filename pattern (`essay-outline-NNN-*.md` vs. existing `NNN-*.md`) distinguishes them; orientation document can name the form change.
- Outline-Production Discipline is anchored conventionally (skill-text instruction in `/rdd-research`), not structurally per-bullet. The Invariant 8 risk that under task load the agent will produce bare claim-listings without explicit Synthesis Bullets is real. Mitigation: the Pyramid Refinement audit operating at the expansion level catches the resulting failure (an Argument-Graph node with no body content development); the failure is named (Outline-Coherence Signal — discipline-suspect path) rather than absorbed silently.
- The Argument-Graph format (structured-bullet hierarchy) imposes a production-time cost the prose form did not: the author must explicitly compose claim/warrant/evidence dependency structure rather than letting prose develop the reasoning implicitly. Mitigation: this cost is the mechanism through which the audit-fidelity advantage is realized — explicit structure requires explicit composition. The cost is bounded — outline form's production cost is comparable to (and in Cycle 018's experience, lower than) prose form's, because outline form does not require connective-tissue prose.
- Cycle 018's evidence base is directional, not statistical. The form change is operationalized on Loop C's structural argument plus Cycle 018's directional empirical signals — not on a multi-cycle production study. Future cycles may surface evidence against the form change; the methodology's response is the standard one (file a superseding ADR if the evidence warrants it).

**Neutral:**

- The form change does not introduce new domain-model invariants. Invariant 8 already governs the structural-vs-conventional anchoring question; this ADR applies Invariant 8 to the form change by separating the layers (structural verification at expansion-fidelity level; conventional discipline at per-bullet level). Backward propagation does not fire.
- The form change is mechanically implementable as a `/rdd-research` skill-text update. BUILD-phase work for the form change consists of the skill-text edits, the corresponding audit-skill amendment (ADR-093), and one or more fixture tests. No new infrastructure required.
- The Cycle 018 outline-form essay (`./docs/essays/017-outlines-as-research-artifacts-outline.md`) becomes a partial precedent for the form. It has three of the four sections (Abstract + Citation-Embedded Outline + References; the Argument-Graph section is the MODEL-gate refinement). Future cycles producing Essay-Outlines have an explicit four-section template; the existing partial precedent does not retroactively require the missing Argument-Graph section.
- Whether the form change's output-side susceptibility-surface reduction during RESEARCH production is offset by input-side susceptibility effects in downstream agents consuming the Essay-Outline (per Kim et al. 2025, structured/citation-anchored inputs inducing higher regressive sycophancy in some agent configurations) is an open empirical question — held as Open Question 7 throughout Cycle 018 and explicitly designated at the DISCOVER gate as "not a DECIDE blocker." Future cycles can test this by comparing downstream-agent behavior on Essay-Outline input versus prose-essay input across a deliberately structured production-comparison protocol. The form change ships with this open question encoded as a scope-of-claim caveat on the net-benefit framing in the Positive consequences.
