# ADR-027: Enrich Framing Conversation with Four-Dimension Navigation

**Status:** Proposed

## Context

The synthesis framing conversation (ADR-013, Phase 3) currently offers a flat repertoire of narrative lenses: the ABT sentence, story spine, braided structure, and volta placement, plus three narrative inversions (ADR-017). Essay 006 ("The Outline as Exhibition") identifies this vocabulary as thin — the lenses operate at different levels of abstraction (ABT is sentence-level; braiding is structural; inversions are framing moves) and the conversation lacks a framework for navigating them.

The essay's research across writing pedagogy (Bascom, Miller & Paola), constraint-based composition (OuLiPo), epistemic posture (Montaigne, Cage), and curation theory (Museum of Jurassic Technology, wunderkammer) reveals that four dimensions simultaneously shape how a synthesis outline should be constructed. These are not sequential steps but simultaneous considerations.

## Decision

The framing conversation navigates four dimensions:

1. **Discovery type** — which novelty signals (ADR-013) are strongest, and what structural affinities do they carry? Explicit surprise suggests narrative-with-a-lift; reframing suggests the whorl of reflection or hermit crab; assumption denial suggests structures that build-then-dismantle; negative case integration suggests braided structure; superseded decisions suggest segmented or dialogue form.

2. **Narrative form** — the shape the telling takes. The vocabulary expands from the current flat list to three tiers: established patterns (braided, segmented, whorl of reflection, lyric, narrative with a lift, dipping into the well, formal limits of focus — from Bascom), hermit crab forms (essay inhabiting a non-essay shell — algorithm, recipe, field guide, proof, dialogue, literate program, exhibition catalog — from Miller & Paola), and meta-forms (Borgesian review, speculative nonfiction, counterfactual). The form is not chosen from a menu; it emerges as the writer discovers what the material demands.

3. **Audience constraint** — audience and medium as creative catalyst, not delivery specification. Each medium imposes constraints that function as creative catalysts (Form-Content Generation). A Story Collider piece demands personal voice; an Ignite talk demands one clear point; explaining to a child demands genuine understanding. The audience may not be known at the start — the material may disclose its audience through composition.

4. **Epistemic posture** — the writer's stance toward certainty. Three positions: determined (know the argument, need the best form — outline as map), exploratory (sense something, need writing to reveal it — outline as invitation, after Montaigne), indeterminate (have elements and relationships, each realization discovers something new — outline as score, after Cage). The posture determines what the outline even *is*.

The existing narrative lenses (ABT, story spine, volta placement) and inversions (ADR-017) remain available as tools within the dimensions — they are not replaced but organized into a richer framework.

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Gives the framing conversation structured range instead of a flat list of tools
- Discovery type → narrative form mapping provides a heuristic starting offer ("Your trail shows a reframing — what if the essay enacted that reframe?") that is more generative than "pick from a list." The agent should present these as one possibility to try, not as what the material definitively suggests, to avoid anchoring the writer on a speculative mapping
- Audience constraint as creative catalyst unlocks content that unconstrained composition misses — the hermit crab principle applied to medium, not just genre
- Epistemic posture makes the outline's nature explicit — a determined outline and an indeterminate outline are different artifacts, and the conversation should know which it is producing
- Preserves ADR-013's three-phase structure and ADR-017's inversions — enriches Phase 3 without restructuring the conversation

**Negative:**
- Four simultaneous dimensions may overwhelm writers who want a simpler conversation — mitigated by the agent navigating dimensions responsively rather than presenting all four upfront
- Discovery type → narrative form mapping is speculative (essay 006 §3 acknowledges this) — the writing pedagogy literature does not explicitly connect discovery types to structural forms
- Audience constraint may be premature if the writer does not yet have a communicative context — mitigated by allowing audience to emerge through composition

**Neutral:**
- The three inversions (ADR-017) become one set of lenses within the narrative form dimension — their governance is unchanged
- The worth-the-calories quality tests (ADR-014) remain as separate assessment — they evaluate the framing's interestingness, not its dimensional completeness
- No invariant changes — the four-dimension model operates within the existing invariant framework
