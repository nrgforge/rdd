# ADR-030: Outline as Two-Register Artifact

**Status:** Proposed

## Context

ADR-013 specifies the outline deliverable as non-formulaic, containing the central question, key turns and threads, opening scene, closing implication, and structural notes, plus pre-populated references verified by citation audit and argument audit. Essay 006 §§6 and 8 identify that the outline actually operates in two registers that serve different functions and are verified by different means.

The exhibition metaphor (essay 006 §6) integrates the essay's findings: the outline is an exhibition design — a curated arrangement of artifacts from the research trail where meaning emerges from selection, juxtaposition, and the spaces between. The Museum of Jurassic Technology's "shimmer" (Weschler) — the productive state between wonder and doubt — is the curatorial register at work.

But curation alone is not sufficient. The argument audit (already prescribed by ADR-013) verifies that claims follow from evidence and framing does not overreach. This logical verification operates on a different register than the curatorial one. An exhibition that "works" is not necessarily one where every claim follows deductively — it is one where the juxtapositions create a coherent emotional and intellectual experience. The synthesis outline holds both.

## Decision

The outline is a two-register artifact:

1. **Argumentative backbone** — the register verified by the argument audit. Claims follow from evidence, framing does not overreach, the volta is grounded in the artifact trail. The argument audit verifies this register. The backbone exists even in lyric, hermit-crab, or indeterminate forms — the logic may be subterranean, but it must be sound. A poem can be logically valid; a recipe can be argumentatively coherent.

2. **Curatorial arrangement** — the curatorial register, layered over the argumentative backbone. Meaning through selection (which artifacts to include and exclude — negative space matters), juxtaposition (proximity creates meaning neither element carries alone), scale shifts (zooming from a variable name to a philosophical implication and back), the shimmer (productive tension left unresolved), and the personal voice (why *these* objects, why *this* arrangement — the curation reveals the curator).

The two registers are complementary, not competing. The argument makes it true; the curation makes it alive. The argument audit checks the first register. No formal mechanism checks the second — curatorial quality is a judgment the writer and agent make together during the framing conversation, informed by structural experiments (ADR-028).

When producing the outline, the agent identifies both registers explicitly:
- The argumentative backbone: the logical thread connecting discoveries to claims to implications
- The curatorial arrangement: how the reader/listener/viewer encounters the ideas, where the scale shifts, what juxtapositions create resonance, where negative space creates meaning

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Makes explicit what ADR-013 implies — the outline has both a logical register and a curatorial register, and they serve different functions
- Resolves the tension between argument audit (which checks logic) and curatorial quality (which operates by association, resonance, and juxtaposition) — both are valid, both are present, they operate on different registers
- Gives the writer a vocabulary for the experiential dimension (selection, juxtaposition, scale shifts, shimmer, negative space, personal voice) that the current skill lacks
- Grounded in curation theory (wunderkammer tradition, Museum of Jurassic Technology, Weschler) — not impressionistic

**Negative:**
- Two registers add conceptual complexity to the outline — mitigated by the registers being complementary, not conflicting, and by the agent handling the structural scaffolding
- The curatorial register has no formal verification mechanism — the agent and writer assess it through judgment during the conversation, which is less reliable than the argument audit for the logical register
- The exhibition metaphor may not resonate with all writers — mitigated by the metaphor being an organizing principle for the agent, not a requirement imposed on the writer's creative process
- An alternative reading is that the argumentative backbone and curatorial arrangement are not two registers of the outline but two lenses for evaluating a single structure. If so, the agent need not produce them as separate layers but should ensure the single outline can be read through both lenses. The current formulation favors the two-register view because it produces more explicit artifacts (the agent identifies both), but this may be operationally heavier than necessary

**Neutral:**
- The argument audit's scope is unchanged — it still checks logical soundness. The curatorial register is explicitly outside its scope
- ADR-013 is not superseded — the outline structure and citation/argument audit requirements remain. This ADR adds structural specificity to what "non-formulaic" means in practice
- The worth-the-calories tests (ADR-014) primarily evaluate the argumentative register — whether the essay denies assumptions, has a genuine turn, challenges existing belief. Curatorial quality is a separate concern, though the two registers interact: a sound argument poorly curated may still fail to engage readers
