# ADR-014: Worth-the-Calories Quality Gate

**Status:** Proposed

## Context

Essay 003 §5 establishes the economic context: there is more writing available than any person can consume, and AI generates competent prose on demand. The synthesis essay competes for reader attention in this environment. The user's reflection (003 reflections §3) sharpened the framing: "If it's not worth reading it's not worth writing." This is authorial discipline, not a system constraint.

The essay identifies three tests that compose into a quality gate, drawing on Murray Davis's assumption-denial framework, Randy Olson's ABT narrative structure, and the inversion principle already operating within RDD.

## Decision

Before committing to the outline, the synthesis conversation applies a quality gate composed of three tests:

**The Davis Test.** Can the writer articulate which widely-held assumption the essay denies? If no assumption is denied — if the essay merely confirms what readers already believe — it is not interesting. The twelve categories of assumption denial (organization, composition, abstraction, generalization, stabilization, function, evaluation, correlation, coexistence, covariation, opposition, causation) provide a vocabulary for characterizing the denial.

**The ABT Test.** Can the essay's central claim be expressed as: "Context AND more context, BUT complication, THEREFORE consequence"? The BUT is the essential element. If the BUT does not land — if the complication is not genuinely surprising — the essay fails.

**The Inversion Test.** Can the essay's central claim be stated as the negation of something the target audience currently believes? If not, the essay is confirming rather than challenging.

**If no framing passes all three tests, the honest answer is: this cycle did not produce an essay worth writing.** This is a valid outcome. Not every RDD cycle generates publishable insight. The gate returns a negative result rather than manufacturing interest where none exists.

**This is authorial discipline, not a system gate.** The agent facilitates the tests during the framing conversation (Phase 3 of ADR-013), but the writer makes the call. The agent does not block or approve — the writer decides whether the standard is met.

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Prevents producing mediocre, assumption-confirming prose that adds to the content flood
- Provides concrete vocabulary for discussing essay quality (not vague "is it interesting?")
- "Not yet" is a valid outcome — preserves intellectual honesty
- Aligns with the synthesis essay's purpose: writing that could not have been generated without the specific experience of the person who went through the process

**Negative:**
- May be discouraging when applied — the writer invests in a synthesis conversation and the gate says "not yet"
- Three tests may feel overly analytical for what is ultimately a creative judgment (mitigated: the tests are lenses, not checklists)

**Neutral:**
- The gate is applied during conversation, not as a formal pass/fail — the writer absorbs the tests and makes their own judgment
