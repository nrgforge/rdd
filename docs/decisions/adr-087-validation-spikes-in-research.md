# ADR-087: Validation Spikes as Research Method — with Grounding Reframe of the Beck Port

**Status:** Proposed

## Context

Reflection 016 surfaced the Beck framing as a methodology counter-position: RDD as a port of Beck's *Tidy First?* time-value-of-money argument to a written-thought asset class running on a compressed feedback horizon via practitioner-dogfooding. The framing functions to justify front-loaded research investment: research artifacts are "earning interest" by being readable and reusable across cycles, similar to how Beck argues tidying earns option value by reducing the cost of subsequent change.

The framing is itself an empirical claim about the payoff structure of deep research artifacts, and the methodology has not tested that claim. The RESEARCH-phase Grounding Reframe surfaced this as Target 2 with explicit deferral to DECIDE: *the validation-spike-in-research methodology amendment ADR must be reframed from "how to implement" to "what is the evidence the artifact investment has the payoff Beck assumes, and what would a spike test?"* The concrete probe carried into the DECIDE inputs: name one specific claim in essay 016 §4.5 a spike could test against the world.

The MODEL gate carried Target 2 forward without alteration, recording it as the natural site for the reframe in this ADR's drafting.

The methodology debt this ADR addresses is the cycle-as-instance reflection (reflection 016, "The Beck framing as methodology self-description"): essay 016 itself rests on five literature reviews and existing field evidence, with no spike or validation against built artifacts. By the user's own Beck-port standard, that is research-not-validation: honest scope-of-claim, but also a deferred test.

This ADR resists the temptation to write "how validation spikes should be implemented" as if the question is procedural. The Target 2 reframe forces the prior question: *does* RDD's written-thought asset class have the payoff structure the Beck port assumes?

## Decision

The methodology adopts **validation spikes as an optional step in research phases**, with the Beck-port framing **explicitly held as an unverified claim** rather than as a load-bearing methodology premise. The decision composes three commitments:

### 1. Grounding Reframe of the Beck port

The Beck framing is **not promoted to a methodology premise** in this cycle. Three specific items separate the conceptual port from a verified payoff claim:

- **The compressed-feedback-horizon claim is unverified.** Beck's argument relies on a feedback horizon long enough that present-value cost differences between cleaner and messier code accrue measurable option value. RDD's claim is that practitioner-dogfooding compresses the horizon enough that artifact reusability across cycles produces the analogous option value. Whether that compression is sufficient — whether RDD artifacts are reused across cycles in ways that recoup their authoring cost — has not been measured. The cycle has anecdotal evidence (Cycle 017's RESEARCH inherits MODEL Amendment 21 vocabulary from Cycle 010+; ADR provenance chains; cross-cycle feed-forward signals) but no systematic test.
- **The reusability assumption is conditional.** Artifact reusability depends on the artifact remaining accurate, the corpus remaining navigable, and the practitioner returning to specific artifacts at specific moments. ADR-074's supersession workflow exists precisely because artifacts go stale; ADR-070 and ADR-085's relocations exist because corpus navigability degrades. The reusability assumption is **conditional on continuous corpus stewardship**, which is itself an investment cost the Beck port does not separately account for.
- **The cycle-as-instance reflection is the strongest available evidence the framing has not yet been tested.** Cycle 017's RESEARCH essay rests on lit-review synthesis. The Outcome Test and bolt-on / felt-additional commitments encoded in ADR-083 are practitioner-articulated but have not been applied at scale to a corpus-restructuring exercise that produced measurable downstream comprehension improvement. The methodology's response (this cycle) treats the lit-review synthesis as research-grade evidence; an external evaluator could legitimately ask what spike would have tested the response against the world before encoding it.

The Grounding Reframe's name for this state: the Beck port is a **useful conceptual frame** for the methodology's investment posture but is **not load-bearing structural evidence** that the investment has the assumed payoff structure. Practitioners adopting RDD on the Beck framing's premise are operating on uncertified evidence about the methodology's value claim.

### 2. Validation spikes — what they would test, what they could not

A validation spike in a research phase would be a small, time-boxed empirical test designed to verify a specific claim the research is about to encode. The Target 2 probe specifies §4.5 as the target section for the concrete-claim test. Two §4.5-sourced examples discharge the probe; one §4.1-sourced example is offered as additional context (extending beyond the probe's scope to illustrate the spike pattern across the essay's other §4 subsections):

- **(§4.5) Spike test for the bolt-on / felt-additional signal as failure-mode signal.** Take a corpus that has accumulated explainer documents over multiple cycles; attempt to identify which were proposed as bolt-on workarounds vs. as genuine purpose-built additions through retrospective practitioner judgment; compare the bolt-on judgments to outcome metrics (subsequent corpus engagement, downstream comprehension reports, modification frequency of the explainer-document target). This tests whether the felt-additional quality is detectable retrospectively in a way that correlates with the failure-mode signal the cycle hypothesizes.
- **(§4.5) Spike test for the brief purpose-built on-ramp earning back its reading cost.** Add a brief 1–2 page on-ramp to a subdirectory of an established corpus targeted at zero-prior-familiarity readers; measure subsequent corpus engagement against a baseline of the same readers without the on-ramp. This tests whether the cognitive-economy claim (the on-ramp's reading cost is repaid in reduced downstream cognitive load) holds in observable behavior.
- **(§4.1, beyond the probe's scope; offered as additional context) Spike test for the companion-file pattern resolving audience-conflation.** Take an artifact in Pattern A (audience-tagged sections within the existing artifact) that meets the Pattern B threshold per ADR-084; restructure it to Pattern B with a companion file; have a zero-prior-familiarity reader engage both versions in counterbalanced order; compare comprehension and information-scent metrics. This tests whether the cycle's diagnostic recalibration produces measurable improvement in the failure mode it claims to address. The probe was anchored to §4.5; this example extends to §4.1 to illustrate the spike pattern's applicability across the essay's other intervention sections.

What validation spikes **could not** test in research phases:

- The cumulative-payoff claim of the Beck port (artifact reusability across cycles produces option value comparable to tidying's option value). Cumulative claims require longitudinal observation across many cycles; a single research-phase spike cannot validate them.
- The methodology's outcome-level invariant (Invariant 0 — practitioner authority). Spikes test specific claims, not the methodology's overarching success criterion.
- Any claim whose failure mode operates at corpus scale (corpus comprehension overload, documentation fatigue). Spike-scale evidence is necessarily local; corpus-scale claims require corpus-scale evidence.

The methodology's honest scope of claim from validation spikes: spikes verify specific claims at the scale at which the spike operates. They do not verify the Beck port's cumulative payoff claim.

### 3. Tightly-scoped prototyping as a research method

The methodology adopts **tightly-scoped prototyping as a research method alongside lit-review, literature search, and research-methods review** — used where prototyping would (a) ground the research in actual interaction such that the essay's claims are answers to questions tested against the world rather than reasoned in the abstract, and (b) act as **counterforce against possibility-space explosion** — narrowing what the essay must commit to by replacing speculative reasoning with tangible answers, before downstream phases inherit (and elaborate on) speculation that YAGNI would prune.

This positioning matters. The framing is not "validation spike as remediation for a Beck-port deficit" (which was the original entry framing the gate sharpened away from). The framing is "tightly-scoped prototyping is one of several research methods, used where appropriate — alongside lit-review, literature search, and methods review — to ground research in actual interaction." The Beck-port reframe in §1 still stands as the cycle's calibration on the methodology's investment posture; this section names how the methodology's research-method toolkit expands in response, not as a patch to a deficit.

Invariant 8's anchoring requirement applies: tightly-scoped prototyping is not an unconditional structural mechanism. The question of whether a cycle's research would benefit from prototyping is judgment-applied, used where appropriate. Mandating prototyping for every research claim would foreclose cycles where the claim's evidence base is already strong, well-grounded, and directly literature-established at the scale the methodology relies on.

The encoding:

- **Trigger question (felt, not mechanical).** Is the cycle at risk of producing speculative claims that downstream phases will over-elaborate, where a tangible answer from a focused prototype would prune the possibility space directly? Or: would interaction-grounding (testing the cycle's claims against an actual built slice) surface real additional questions or directions that lit-review and methods alone cannot reach? When either applies, the cycle considers a prototype. Cycle 017's diagnostic recalibration on context-mismatch — drives ADR-083 / ADR-084 / ARCHITECT restructuring at considerable scope without having tested the recalibration against an interactional artifact — would have been a strong candidate under this trigger.
- **Decision moment.** The trigger fires at the research → discover gate. The practitioner asks: "what's the simplest thing we could build and use to surface real questions or directions before the essay's claims become structural commitments downstream?" If a prototype is warranted, the cycle pauses RESEARCH advancement until the prototype completes and its findings are integrated into the essay; if not, the cycle proceeds with an explicit honest scope-of-claim recording the choice.
- **Documentation.** A research phase that considers and rejects prototyping records the rejection with rationale (e.g., "the literature support is direct on this exact scenario at the scale the methodology relies on; the claims have been tested in cited research; interaction-grounding would not surface additional questions or directions"). The rejection is visible, not silent. A research phase that runs a prototype documents the prototype's design, what it tested, what it surfaced (questions, directions, refinements), and what it updates in the essay.
- **Skill-text encoding.** The `/rdd-research` skill gains an explicit step at the research → discover gate where the trigger question is named and the practitioner decides prototype-or-no-prototype with rationale recorded. The skill text does not enforce prototyping (Invariant 8 disqualifies that as unconditional); it surfaces the question at a step-anchored position.

### 3a. Anti-elaboration positioning

Tightly-scoped prototyping joins the methodology's family of anti-elaboration mechanisms — different phases, same disposition: prefer tangible answers and pruned scope to speculative reasoning and accumulated possibility-space.

| Mechanism | Phase | Role in this family (with primary disposition noted where it differs) |
|---|---|---|
| Tightly-scoped prototyping (this ADR) | RESEARCH | Prune speculative claims at source by grounding them in actual interaction. Anti-elaboration is the primary disposition. |
| Tidy First / YAGNI (Beck) | BUILD | Build only what the next test requires; resist speculative generality. Anti-elaboration is the primary disposition (Beck's framing). |
| ADR-077 Applicability Check | BUILD | Test pattern reuse against the new context's specific constraints. Note: ADR-077's primary disposition is **anti-Einstellung** (attentional suppression at the pattern-reuse moment, per Bilalic et al. 2008); its anti-elaboration function is secondary, not its primary purpose. Included here at the family-resemblance level — the mechanism shares the "prefer tangible answers to speculative reasoning" disposition with the others — but its main job is shifting attention, not pruning elaboration. |

The connection makes a held-open future-cycle candidate (MODEL Amendment 21: *"Does RDD over-engineer? Does RDD's research front-loading produce systems whose elaborated possibility space exceeds what the user actually needs?"*) partly addressable in-cycle: tightly-scoped prototyping is the research-phase mechanism that resists possibility-space explosion at the source, before it propagates downstream into ADRs and system design that elaborate on speculation. The future-cycle question is not foreclosed — the broader critique of RDD's structural posture remains worth a dedicated cycle — but the in-cycle mechanism is now named.

### 4. Cycle-as-instance reflection as a methodology-level standing caveat

The cycle-as-instance reflection — *any cycle's findings are research-not-validation until something has been built and tested against the world* — is encoded as a **standing caveat on the corpus**. The reflection appears in the methodology's scope-of-claim language (ADR-069) as a load-bearing limitation of research-phase outputs:

> Research-phase essays and ADRs reflect the cycle's understanding at the time of authoring, audited against literature and conversation. They have not necessarily been validated against built artifacts at the scale the methodology operates. Cycles that adopt validation spikes (per ADR-087) have stronger evidence for specific claims; cycles that operate at lit-review-and-reflection scope have research-grade evidence at that scope.

The standing caveat does not foreclose lit-review-and-reflection cycles — the methodology is designed to admit them at appropriate scopes. It names the evidence ceiling such cycles achieve, so practitioners and downstream phases inherit findings with calibrated confidence rather than as validated claims.

## Rejected alternatives

**Promote the Beck port to a methodology premise.** Rejected. The cycle's evidence does not support the cumulative-payoff claim at the level a methodology premise would require. Promoting the framing without evidence would itself be the failure mode the Target 2 reframe surfaced — research feeling productive while deferring testing against the world.

**Reject the Beck port entirely as unsupported.** Rejected. The framing is a useful conceptual lens for the methodology's investment posture; rejecting it entirely would discard a frame practitioners use to reason about why front-loaded research investment is worthwhile. The reframe is calibration, not rejection.

**Mandate validation spikes for every research-phase claim (unconditional structural mechanism).** Rejected. Invariant 8's anchoring requirement disqualifies mechanisms whose firing is judgment-applied without a step-anchored trigger. Mandating spikes for every claim is judgment-applied (which claims warrant spikes is itself a judgment) and would foreclose lit-review-only cycles where the evidence base is already strong. The optional-step encoding with a step-anchored decision moment is the structurally honest form.

**Mandate validation spikes only for claims that contradict prior cycles or the literature.** Considered. A contradiction-only trigger is more mechanical than the §3 reframed trigger (interaction-grounding rationale + counterforce against possibility-space explosion), but rejected because it does not capture the gate-articulated rationale: a claim does not need to contradict prior commitments to warrant prototyping; it warrants prototyping when interaction-grounding would surface real additional questions or when the cycle is at risk of producing speculative claims that downstream phases will over-elaborate. The §3 reframed trigger captures both contradictions and load-bearing extensions where over-elaboration is the risk; the contradiction-only trigger captures only the former.

**Encode validation spikes as Tier 1 unconditional with the trigger condition as the structural anchor.** Considered. The trigger condition could be argued to be step-anchored (it fires at the research → discover gate). Rejected because the trigger question (per §3 reframed: "is the cycle at risk of producing speculative claims downstream phases will over-elaborate? would interaction-grounding surface additional questions or directions?") is felt rather than mechanically observable. Anticipating downstream over-elaboration risk requires the practitioner's judgment of cycle scope and consequence; recognizing where interaction-grounding would surface additional content requires the practitioner's judgment of what lit-review and methods alone can reach. Both are judgment moves; mandating prototype-or-justified-rejection at the gate would convert the optional-step encoding into a ceremonial-compliance ritual rather than a substantive research method. The optional-step encoding with rationale recording is the honest form.

**Defer the Beck-port reframe and the validation-spike encoding to a future cycle.** Rejected. The Target 2 reframe was committed at the RESEARCH-phase Grounding Reframe with explicit DECIDE deferral. Deferring further would silently leave Target 2 unaddressed. The cycle's resolution at this gate is the appropriate site for the reframe.

## Consequences

**Positive:**

- The Beck port is calibrated rather than promoted. The methodology's investment posture remains conceptually grounded but does not claim verified payoff structure. Practitioners adopting RDD have honest scope-of-claim about the framing's evidence base.
- Validation spikes become an available tool for research phases that need them, without becoming a mandatory ritual that lit-review-only cycles must perform ceremonially.
- The cycle-as-instance reflection is encoded as a standing caveat, surfacing the evidence ceiling lit-review-only cycles achieve. Future cycles inherit a corpus with calibrated confidence on prior-cycle claims.
- The Target 2 reframe is discharged. The DECIDE-deferred RESEARCH Grounding Reframe is addressed at this gate with a substantive disposition rather than a ceremonial nod.
- The optional-step encoding satisfies Invariant 8 honestly — the mechanism's structural status (Tier 2 best-effort) matches its actual character (judgment-applied, gate-positioned, rationale-recorded).

**Negative:**

- The Beck port reframe may dissatisfy practitioners who adopted RDD partly on the framing's promised payoff structure. The reframe does not deliver the framing as verified evidence; it explicitly names the framing as not-yet-tested. Mitigation: the reframe is calibration, not rejection — practitioners can continue to use the framing as a conceptual lens while holding its empirical claim as unverified.
- The optional-step encoding leaves practitioners with judgment burden at the research → discover gate. Practitioners must decide whether their cycle's claims warrant spike validation, with no mechanical rule. Mitigation: the trigger condition's three named criteria (load-bearing × literature-not-direct × evidence-anecdotal) provide felt indicators; the rationale-recording requirement creates a record of judgment that future cycles can review and learn from.
- The standing caveat may seem like a global disclaimer that weakens the methodology's claims. Mitigation: the caveat is honest scope-of-claim rather than a disclaimer — research-phase artifacts are explicitly research-grade evidence at their stated scope; the caveat names that scope rather than diminishing it.
- This ADR itself is encoded without spike validation. The cycle-as-instance reflection applies to this ADR as it applies to the rest of the cycle's outputs. The standing caveat the ADR encodes also applies to the ADR.

**Neutral:**

- The decision is a methodology amendment, not a domain-model amendment. The vocabulary (Validation Spike, Cycle-as-Instance Reflection) is encoded at methodology-principle level here; whether to add it as domain-model concepts is a future-cycle question.
- The ADR does not specify the spike's format, time-boxing, or evaluation criteria beyond the three example claims in §2. Future cycles that run spikes will develop the format through practice; this ADR commits to the trigger condition and the optional-step encoding.
- The skill-text encoding (a step at the research → discover gate) is a concrete edit to `/rdd-research` that is part of this ADR's BUILD-phase scope.
