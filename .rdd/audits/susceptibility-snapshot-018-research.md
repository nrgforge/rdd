## Interpretation

### Pattern

Two signals converge on a clear pattern: the cycle's argumentative backbone crystallized around the user's entry framing before the research loop independently examined alternatives. The constraint-removal response — which is ADR-082's designed solution to exactly this problem — partially misfired. It bracketed the right artifact (the prose essay form) and failed to bracket the proposed replacement (outline form), so the solution space narrowed from "any artifact form" to "outline vs. prose essay" at the entry moment. The research loops then mostly confirmed this narrowing rather than reopened it.

This pattern has a specific structural signature distinct from previous cycles. In Cycle 017's research phase (snapshot-017-research.md), the framing-adoption signals were distributed across multiple inflection points mid-cycle (cognitive-economy constraint at R2, Beck framing at the gate). In Cycle 018, the dominant framing-adoption event is concentrated at the entry — the constraint-removal moment — after which the cycle is not particularly susceptible. The audit infrastructure functioned: the research-design reviewer caught the embedded conclusion at Step 1.3, the loop subagents operated in isolation, and the audit iterations produced genuine corrections. The pattern is best described as a front-loaded framing commitment that the subsequent research confirmed rather than challenged.

### Earned Confidence vs. Sycophantic Reinforcement

The cycle has genuine earned-confidence characteristics:

- The Loop B reframing finding is a real competing frame that was surfaced and built on rather than suppressed. The cycle's conclusion that the outline move cannot be primarily justified as critique-response is counter to the entry motivation; the cycle moved away from the user's simpler framing and toward a more constrained set of justifications. This is the opposite of sycophantic reinforcement.
- The Spike S1 evidence is internally generated by isolated subagents and includes a genuine mixed finding: the 80–85% fidelity figure encompasses both gains and losses, and the loss dimension (differential-confidence calibration) is presented as a real fidelity concern, not minimized.
- The dogfooded citation comparison produced asymmetric results that support the cycle's thesis. This is either evidence-construction or evidence-discovery — the question the dispatch prompt names directly.
- The audit iterations introduced new issues at each pass (the "correction pass introduces 3-5 new issues" pattern is documented in the dispatch), which is consistent with genuine fidelity improvement rather than with a cycle that found what it was looking for and stopped.

The primary sycophancy-consistent signal is the structural one: the constraint-removal response converged on outline form before the research loop ran. The three justifications the cycle settled on (sycophancy-resistance, CRESS-Small alignment, reduced rhetorical-vs-logical conflation) are all justifications for outline form specifically, rather than for susceptibility-surface-minimization in general. The prior-art alternatives that might have satisfied susceptibility-surface-minimization via different forms — particularly the Cochrane hybrid, which the Loop C lit-review explicitly called the "gold standard" for the research question — are adjudicated against in §3 but are not carried forward as live competitors in the cycle's central argument. The Cochrane hybrid is dismissed via a scale-incompatibility argument; the R2 prose argument audit flagged that this framing choice weights novelty (ARA, a 2026 preprint) over maturity (Cochrane, a decades-refined methodology) in a way that is arguable.

The honest characterization: this cycle produced evidence that convergently supports its entry hypothesis. The convergence is consistent with genuine evidence discovery — the research loops were structured to surface disconfirming evidence, and some was surfaced (the Loop B reframing finding, the 80–85% fidelity loss dimension, the Kim et al. input-side susceptibility complication). The convergence is also consistent with a cycle that, because its entry framing was absorbed before the research ran, was designed to test rather than discover. The cycle-as-instance caveat (ADR-087 §4) is the appropriate epistemic stance; the dispatch prompt is right that the question is live.

### The self-referential meta-observation

The prose argument auditor's meta-observation warrants specific attention: "The essay's own central argument is that prose form is more susceptible to framing-adoption than outline form, because prose embeds framing invisibly in connective tissue. This audit identifies two instances where the prose form of the essay adopted framings from the research log without surfacing available alternatives... This is consistent with the essay's thesis rather than a refutation of it — but it is an instance of the phenomenon the essay describes occurring in the artifact the essay inhabits."

This observation is important not as a refutation of the thesis but as a confirmation of the evaluation apparatus's integrity: the framing audit caught instances of the phenomenon the essay argues for. This is the mechanism working. What it also confirms for this snapshot's purposes is that the prose artifact is itself susceptible in the way the essay predicts prose artifacts are susceptible — the framing-adoption events in the prose form were not caught by the cycle's own revision passes; they were caught by the isolated argument auditor. The outline form's R2 framing audit produced zero new framing findings of this type. This is directional signal, not statistical proof.

### What the iterative-audit pattern tells us

The pattern — each correction pass introduces new issues that subsequent audits catch — is documented in the dispatch and is present in the data: the prose artifact went from 4P1 + 7P2 + 9P3 at citation R1 to 0P1 + 2P2 + 2P3 at argument R2, with corrections at each stage introducing 3-5 new issues. Two readings are possible. First: the iterative audit is a genuine error-correction process converging on a more accurate artifact — this is methodology evidence that the audit infrastructure works. Second: the iterative audit is an error-introduction-and-correction loop that produces the appearance of rigor while the underlying framing choices accumulate corrections around a fixed center. The data does not cleanly separate these. The corrections are real corrections (confirmed landed, not just acknowledged). The framing choices persisted across the audit cycle — the dominant framing (susceptibility-surface-reduction first) did not change from R1 to R2 in either artifact. This is consistent with interpretation one (framing is correct, details are being refined) and consistent with interpretation two (framing is fixed, auditing around the edges).

## Specific Findings Relevant to a Grounding Reframe

Three specific concerns emerge from the material and warrant naming before downstream phases inherit them.

### Finding 1: The constraint-removal protocol misfired at its most important moment

The research-design reviewer diagnosed this at Step 1.3, and the research plan was amended to compensate (Loop C expanded, R1 task added). What the reviewer could not fix is the first-mover advantage the outline hypothesis acquired: the research loops investigated outline form as the lead hypothesis, not as one of several candidates the research was designed to discover. The evidence produced by Loop C (including the Cochrane hybrid as "gold standard" and the ARA as "directional evidence") was evaluated against outline form's candidacy, not allowed to generate independent candidate forms. DECIDE will inherit an artifact that argues convincingly for outline form; it will not inherit an artifact that independently derived outline form as the best available option after holding the form question open.

**What downstream phases would build on without grounding:** DECIDE's ADR on the RESEARCH artifact form will be drafted against an argumentative corpus that treats the outline-vs-prose comparison as having been empirically tested (it was — one cycle, one essay, one spike) and theoretically grounded (it was — via CRESS, sycophancy literature, and Loop C). The alternative framing — that the form question may be less consequential than the discipline question, and that a strong argument-audit mechanism on prose could achieve the same susceptibility reduction without artifact-form migration — is available in the R2 prose framing audit's Alternative C and is not foregrounded in either artifact's conclusions.

### Finding 2: The CRESS scope-transfer is a working hypothesis the cycle built on as though it were established

The affirmative case for CRESS scope-transfer (coding-task context → methodology-phase artifacts) is an analogy the essay explicitly labels as "the cycle's working hypothesis" and "not validated by Gorman directly." The §2 qualification is present and correct. However, by §7's co-illumination synthesis, the scope-transfer is treated as established — the "two independent paths reaching the same diagnosis" conclusion depends on CRESS Thread 2 being an independent path, which requires accepting the scope transfer. If the transfer is contested, Thread 2 is not independent evidence but a second application of the user's susceptibility-surface framing via a framework that was selected because it happens to share that framing's priorities. The Loop A scope-transfer examination is the right mechanism for this; the cycle ran it and produced the affirmative case; the issue is that the co-illumination finding's weight depends on treating two threads as genuinely independent when they share an analogy the cycle constructed.

**What downstream phases would build on without grounding:** If DECIDE drafts an ADR using the two-thread convergence as justification for a methodology change, the convergence's strength is a function of the scope-transfer's validity. An ADR that encodes "two independent frameworks support this change" is stronger than an ADR that encodes "one framework, applied twice via an analogy." The difference matters for how DECIDE should weight the CRESS findings relative to the practice-based evidence.

### Finding 3: The evidence-construction vs. evidence-discovery question on the dogfooded citation comparison

The cycle produced both artifact forms and then submitted them to isolated citation audits, which returned results asymmetrically favoring outline form (3 unique-to-prose P1 errors, 0 unique-to-outline P1 errors). The essay treats this as empirical discovery (§5: "the audits' findings are themselves Cycle 018 data... an empirical signal the cycle produced about itself"). The dispatch prompt correctly names the alternative: "did the evidence genuinely converge, or did the cycle systematically construct evidence that converged?"

The specific mechanism to examine: the prose form was produced by a subagent that synthesized the research log into prose narrative, requiring re-attribution moves (rewording bibliographic entries, restating attributions in flowing text) at synthesis time. The outline form was produced by a subagent that structured the same material into citation-bearing bullets, requiring attribution at claim-level rather than at synthesis-narrative level. The re-attribution mechanism in prose synthesis is the essay's proposed explanation for why prose introduces more citation errors. This explanation is plausible. It is also a post-hoc rationalization of a measurement from a single instance. The cycle acknowledges this (§5 calibrations: "The evidence base is single-cycle and small"). What the cycle does not acknowledge is that the production method for the prose form (synthesis-from-research-log) is the specific thing being tested for susceptibility, so the comparison tests synthesis-from-log-to-prose vs. structuring-from-log-to-outline — not prose-in-general vs. outline-in-general. If prose produced directly from primary sources rather than synthesized from a research log shows fewer citation errors, the unique-to-prose P1 findings are not properties of prose form per se but of the specific prose-production method used in this cycle.

**What downstream phases would build on without grounding:** DECIDE's form-change ADR will be supported by a citation-error count comparison that the artifacts present as evidence for outline form's superiority. The comparison is real but its generalizability is scoped to the prose-production method this cycle used (synthesis from research log). An ADR that encodes "outline form has fewer citation errors than prose form" without specifying that the comparison is between synthesis-from-log-to-prose and structure-from-log-to-outline may overstate the generalizability.

## Recommendation

**Grounding Reframe recommended** — three named targets, in priority order.

### Target 1 (Primary): The constraint-removal protocol's first-mover advantage for outline form

**Uncertainty:** Whether outline form was derived as the best available option after holding the artifact-form question open, or was absorbed as the entry hypothesis and then confirmed by the research loop.

**Grounding action:** Before DECIDE drafts a form-change ADR, the user should be asked: given the full evidence produced by the cycle — the Loop C findings, the Cochrane hybrid's "gold standard" verdict in the lit-review, the Spike S1 80–85% preservation finding, the citation comparison — is there a different artifact form (the Cochrane hybrid's layered-audit-with-structure approach; the structured-abstract-plus-synthesis hybrid; audit-extended prose with mandated explicit calibration bullets) that would satisfy the susceptibility-surface-minimization goal as well as outline form does? If the answer is "yes, but outline is simpler and available now," that is a decision DECIDE should encode explicitly as the rationale, not inherit as a given. If the answer is "no, the evidence distinguishes outline form from these alternatives," then DECIDE's ADR can cite the specific evidence that rules them out — which the current artifacts provide partially but not comprehensively.

**What would be built on without grounding:** DECIDE would draft a form-change ADR treating outline form as the supported answer from research without encoding that the question was entered with outline as the hypothesis, that the constraint-removal protocol absorbed the hypothesis before bracketing it, and that the alternatives were adjudicated against a hypothesis the research was designed to confirm rather than against a null field.

### Target 2 (Secondary): The CRESS scope-transfer as working hypothesis vs. established premise

**Uncertainty:** Whether the two-thread convergence (practice + CRESS principle) represents two genuinely independent paths to the same diagnosis, or one path (susceptibility-surface-minimization) applied via two different frameworks, one of which (CRESS) was selected and scope-transferred specifically because it shares the path's central framing.

**Grounding action:** Before DECIDE drafts any ADR that cites the two-thread convergence as justification, the user should be asked to examine what the CRESS thread contributes that the practice thread does not. If CRESS independently identifies RESEARCH as the anomalous phase via mechanisms that do not depend on the susceptibility-surface framing (e.g., purely via the S-Small principle applied without susceptibility assumptions), then the convergence is genuine. If the Loop A scope-transfer argument works only because RDD research artifacts are treated as "context" in CRESS's sense, and if that treatment is itself a susceptibility-surface framing choice, then CRESS is not an independent second path. The question is whether Thread 2 could have identified RESEARCH as the anomalous phase without Thread 1's framing having been in the room. The research log does not document the Loop D synthesis running before the constraint-removal response; it documents the constraint-removal response shaping the research plan that then included Loop D. The sequencing matters for the independence claim.

**What would be built on without grounding:** A form-change ADR citing "two independent frameworks converge on RESEARCH as the anomalous phase" would be claiming stronger evidential support than the cycle warrants if the convergence depends on a shared analogy rather than on independent mechanisms.

### Target 3 (Tertiary): The citation-comparison evidence scope

**Uncertainty:** Whether the unique-to-prose citation errors reflect a property of prose form in general, or a property of the specific prose-production method used in this cycle (synthesis from research log with re-attribution at synthesis time).

**Grounding action:** DECIDE's form-change ADR should note explicitly that the citation-comparison evidence is scoped to one cycle's prose-production method, not to prose form in general. The ADR can still cite the comparison as evidence; the question is what claim the evidence actually supports. The practical implication: if a future cycle produces prose directly from primary sources rather than via research-log synthesis, the comparison should be re-run before the citation-error advantage of outline form is treated as a settled finding.

**What would be built on without grounding:** A form-change ADR citing "outline form has fewer citation errors than prose form" without the scope qualification may be cited by future cycles as evidence for a claim broader than the evidence established, and may close off the prose production method as an alternative without the comparison having tested it.
