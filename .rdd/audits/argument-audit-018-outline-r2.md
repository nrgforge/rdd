### New Issues Found

#### P2 — Should Fix

**Issue P2-new-1 — §11.3 Open Question 7 creates a cross-reference mismatch with §5.7's SCOPE QUALIFICATION**

- **Location:** §5.7 SCOPE QUALIFICATION (at point of invocation) and §11.3 Open Question 7
- **Claim:** §5.7 says: "Whether outline-structured inputs to DECIDE/ARCHITECT agents trigger Kim et al.-type over-weighting is Open Question 7 (§11.3)." §11.3 Open Question 7 reads: "Whether outline-structured inputs trigger Kim et al. (2025)-type over-weighting in downstream RDD agents (DECIDE, ARCHITECT, BUILD) consuming the outline as input. Kim et al.'s finding that authoritative-structured arguments trigger maximum regressive sycophancy operates at the input side; the cycle's susceptibility-reduction argument operates at the output side. The two-sided question is whether the output-side gain is offset by an input-side loss when outlines are consumed downstream."
- **Issue:** The cross-reference is functional, but §5.7's SCOPE QUALIFICATION does not name BUILD as one of the downstream agents where this question applies — it lists "DECIDE, ARCHITECT, BUILD" — whereas the main body of the §5.7 SCOPE QUALIFICATION only mentions "DECIDE, ARCHITECT, BUILD" in the full parenthetical but then abbreviates to "downstream agents" in the conclusion clause. This is not internally contradictory, but the §5.7 SCOPE QUALIFICATION also frames the Kim et al. effect as an "input side" phenomenon and the artifact design question as "output side" — consistent with the prior audit's framing — but does not explicitly state that the cycle's three convergent arguments (sycophancy-resistance, CRESS-Empirical, reduced rhetorical-vs-logical conflation) address the output side only. A reader who accepts the output/input distinction but then asks "does the cycle's sycophancy-resistance argument hold on the output side?" still needs to cross-reference §11.1 Dimension (a) for the answer. The §5.7 SCOPE QUALIFICATION closes the objection on the input side but does not direct the reader to where the output-side argument is fully made. Minor readability gap.
- **Recommendation:** Add a forward reference at the close of §5.7's SCOPE QUALIFICATION: "The cycle's sycophancy-resistance argument for the output side is operationalized in §11.1 Dimension (a)." This makes the two-sided treatment navigable without additional cross-section hunting.

**Issue P2-new-2 — The §3.5 Q3 answer's CALIBRATION bullet describes co-illumination as a "methodological signal — when an independent framework reaches the same conclusion as practice," but the positive-case warrant (WARRANT 4, added in this revision) changes the epistemic weight of the signal without updating the CALIBRATION**

- **Location:** §3.5 CALIBRATION and WARRANT 4
- **Claim:** §3.5 CALIBRATION reads: "The convergence is a methodological signal — when an independent framework reaches the same conclusion as practice, that is the structural condition under which a methodology change should be considered." WARRANT 4 (added in revision) makes a stronger positive claim: "the precision of convergence — not just same conclusion but same locus and same remediation site — is the basis for 'strongly.'"
- **Issue:** WARRANT 4 argues that the convergence is more than a structural condition (the CALIBRATION's framing) — it is convergence on locus and remediation site, which the document treats as the basis for "strongly." But the CALIBRATION bullet still frames the convergence as "the structural condition under which a methodology change should be considered" — a threshold statement rather than an affirmative strength claim. The CALIBRATION reads as calibrating the convergence downward ("this is when to consider change") while WARRANT 4 argues upward ("this precision is why strongly"). These are not inconsistent, but they pull in different rhetorical directions and a reader reconciling them must infer that CALIBRATION is setting a minimum threshold while WARRANT 4 is arguing the evidence clears it by a significant margin. That inference is not made explicit.
- **Recommendation:** Add a linking clause to §3.5 CALIBRATION: "The convergence is a methodological signal — when an independent framework reaches the same conclusion as practice, that is the structural condition under which a methodology change should be considered. WARRANT 4 argues this convergence clears that threshold with precision (same locus, same remediation site, not merely same conclusion), which is the basis for 'strongly' in the Q3 answer." Alternatively, the CALIBRATION could be retargeted to scope the co-illumination finding rather than to calibrate the threshold, allowing WARRANT 4 to carry the strength argument alone.

### Question 2: What truths were available but not featured?

The revision addressed the two material omissions the prior audit identified (DISCOVER/MODEL S-Small weakness in §3.3; Kim et al. two-sidedness). A check against the source material for newly available truths surfaced one observation:

**Observation: The §6.5 DERIVATION sub-bullet introduces "twelve comparative findings" as the denominator for the 80–85% figure, but the §6.2 comparison summary table shows the prose audit had 8 issues (1 P1, 4 P2, 3 P3) and the outline audit had 10 (1 P1, 4 P2, 5 P3).** The denominator "twelve" is not the total issue count of either audit; per the prior audit's explanation, it is the auditor's count of comparative findings in the Spike S1 Section 3 assessment. The DERIVATION sub-bullet names this correctly ("Spike S1 auditor's Section 3 assessment — nine of twelve comparative findings"). But a reader comparing the DERIVATION bullet against the §6.2 table will see issue counts of 8 and 10, not 12, and will not immediately understand why the denominator is 12 rather than 8 or 10. The DERIVATION sub-bullet explains "twelve comparative findings" without explaining that these are a distinct count from the §6.2 issue totals (the auditor's Section 3 compared findings including paired ones that appeared in different configurations in each form). This creates a small residual opacity.

**Assessment:** This is a P3-level framing precision gap — not an omission of available truth, but an underspecification of the counting basis. It is flagged below.

The revision does not change the dominant framing.
