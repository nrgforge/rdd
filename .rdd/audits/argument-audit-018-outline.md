**Issue 1.1 — "Single largest" superlative in §3.3 is not established by the matrix evidence**

- **Location:** §3.3, OBSERVATION 1 and §3.4 table; echoed in §9 SYNTHESIS
- **Claim:** "RESEARCH is the methodology's single largest S-Small divergence point." In §3.4: "The RESEARCH essay-form divergence is the methodology's largest accidental CRESS-divergence."

- **Recommendation:** Either (a) soften to "RESEARCH is a significant and anomalous S-Small divergence point — the only phase that produces arbitrarily large artifacts as a design outcome rather than as an accumulation side-effect," and explain why DISCOVER/MODEL's accretion is structurally different (those phases grow across cycles through accumulation, not within a single artifact); or (b) add a brief WARRANT bullet explaining that MODEL's WEAK is a principled accretion (domain models are inherently accumulative), DISCOVER's WEAK is mixed-principled, and only RESEARCH's WEAK is fully accidental — thereby grounding "largest accidental divergence" rather than "largest overall divergence." ## Section 2: Framing Audit

### Question 1: What alternative framings did the evidence support?

**Alternative framing A — The outline as a practitioner-hygiene response to the unread-essay problem, not a susceptibility argument.**

The research log records the practitioner's entry motivation clearly: "The 5,000+ word essay format is rarely read end-to-end by the practitioner; it functions primarily as audit substrate and downstream provenance source rather than as a human-read deliverable." This observation — essays are not read — could have been the cycle's dominant framing. Under this framing, the outline is proposed not because it reduces sycophancy surface or improves CRESS alignment, but because it matches the actual use pattern: if no one reads the essay, produce an artifact whose form matches the downstream use (audit substrate, provenance source). The susceptibility-surface argument would be a secondary rationale, not the primary frame.

What would the reader need to believe for this framing to be right? That the primary problem with prose essays is practitioner-adoption failure, and that structural susceptibility is a secondary concern. The evidence supporting this framing: the practitioner-stated entry observation (research log §1); R2's finding that downstream phases consume structured content; the SYNTHESIZE comparison (SYNTHESIZE already outputs outlines because no one reads intermediate synthesis prose either).

The outline's chosen framing (susceptibility-surface as primary) is arguably stronger logically — it grounds the proposal in evidence about AI-artifact behavior rather than practitioner habit. But the unread-essay framing is the one the practitioner actually stated at entry, and it is underrepresented in the outline's argument structure, appearing as a CONTEXT bullet in §1 rather than as a primary framing alternative considered and rejected.

**Alternative framing B — The outline as a temporary workaround pending ARA-class infrastructure.**

Liu et al. (2026) describe a limiting-case architecture that delivers 72.4% → 93.7% QA accuracy gains. Under an ARA-dominant framing, the outline-with-inline-citations is not the answer to the artifact-form question but a step toward the answer — the best RDD can do with current infrastructure. This framing would orient the outline as a transitional form, explicitly provisional, with the cycle's contribution being to identify the need for ARA-like properties and demonstrate a low-infrastructure approximation.

What would the reader need to believe for this framing to be right? That the outline's advantages derive primarily from its machine-traversability properties (claim-evidence separation, explicit provenance nodes), and that these properties are continuous with the ARA's typed claim-evidence bindings, not categorically different. The outline's framing presents the ARA as an interesting limiting case (§5.8) but does not adopt the stronger position that the outline IS a proto-ARA. The source material (Liu et al., the research log's characterization of the ARA as "a step toward ARA-like properties using only existing RDD infrastructure") supports this framing explicitly but it is underweighted in the outline's synthesis.

**Alternative framing C — The CRESS audit as the primary research finding, with the artifact-form question as a downstream implication.**

Thread 2 (CRESS phase-by-phase audit) is presented as independently reaching the same conclusion as Thread 1, which establishes co-illumination. An alternative framing would treat Thread 2 as the primary research contribution: a systematic audit of the methodology against an external principled framework, with artifact-form implications following. Under this framing, the contribution of Cycle 018 is the CRESS audit itself — the methodology's most thorough external-framework alignment analysis — with the outline-form recommendation as one of several downstream implications.

What would the reader need to believe? That the CRESS audit is more durable and broadly useful than the specific artifact-form conclusion, which may be settled by DECIDE-phase deliberation. The outline's chosen framing (Q1 and Q2 co-illuminate, both pointing at the same conclusion) is deliberate: it prevents Thread 2 from being dismissed as scope-overreach. But it also means the CRESS audit's other findings (C-Current's multi-strategy satisfaction, S-Specific's universal strength, BUILD's maximal R/E coverage) receive brief cross-cutting treatment (§3.4 cross-cutting observations) rather than being positioned as findings of independent interest.

### Question 2: What truths were available but not featured?

**Available truth A — DISCOVER also scores WEAK on S-Small, and MODEL scores WEAK on S-Small, but neither receives the "accidental divergence" treatment applied to RESEARCH.**

The research log's Loop D matrix explicitly records DISCOVER as WEAK on S-Small ("accumulates over cycles") and MODEL as WEAK on S-Small ("grows monotonically"). The outline's §3.4 principled-vs-accidental table addresses DISCOVER (classified as "Mixed") and MODEL (classified as "Principled"), which is appropriate analysis. However, the body of §3.3 OBSERVATION 1 presents "RESEARCH is the single largest S-Small divergence point" without surfacing the fact that two other phases are also WEAK on S-Small. A reader of §3.3 alone would not know this. The table in §3.4 is the only place this appears, and the prose flow from §3.3 to §3.4 does not direct the reader to check the table before accepting the §3.3 superlative. This omission partially drives Issue 1.1 above; it is also a framing issue about which phases bear the diagnostic's weight.

Why might it have been underweighted? The outline's argument requires RESEARCH to be the single locus of the problem, to support the claim that Cycle 018 is the methodology's examination of a specific accidental default rather than a systematic CRESS-alignment problem across phases. Including DISCOVER and MODEL's WEAK ratings prominently would complicate the diagnostic focus without (correctly, per §3.4) changing the accidental-vs-principled classification.

Would its inclusion change the argument? Not the conclusion, but the precision of the claim: "RESEARCH is the only phase whose S-Small divergence is fully accidental and fully remediable by form change" is a more accurate and more defensible version of "single largest."

**Available truth B — The Kim et al. (2025) mechanism (citation-based rebuttal triggering highest regressive sycophancy) is invoked at §5.7 as evidence AGAINST audit-extended prose, but the same mechanism creates a potential risk FOR outline form.**

The research log and Loop C both note: Kim et al. (2025) found that models over-weight authoritative-sounding argument structure even when conclusions contradict ground truth. The outline uses this finding to argue that audit-extended prose is susceptible (§5.7 CRITICAL FLAW: authoritative-looking framework without separately auditable claims). But the same mechanism applies to a highly structured, authority-signaling outline: an outline with explicit CLAIM, EVIDENCE, WARRANT, CALIBRATION tags may itself present as a particularly authoritative-structured document that downstream agents over-weight. If downstream agent consumption of the RESEARCH artifact is the concern (Q4), and Kim et al. show that authority-structured arguments trigger the highest sycophancy, then the outline's explicit structural authority-signaling could increase, not decrease, downstream agent susceptibility.

The outline does not acknowledge this tension. The closest acknowledgment is the Loop C synthesis note that "this effect operates at the input side (how models receive arguments), whereas the artifact-design question is about the output side" — distinguishing input susceptibility (how agents read arguments) from output susceptibility (how agents produce artifacts). This distinction is valid and is present in the source (Loop C, §5.7 WARRANT). But the outline does not foreground it as a finding that moderates the Kim et al. use; it is embedded in a WARRANT bullet under §5.7. A reader using Kim et al. to evaluate the outline form itself would encounter a gap.

Would its inclusion change the argument? It would require a more explicit treatment of the input/output distinction at the point where Kim et al. is invoked, acknowledging the two-sided nature of the finding rather than using it only on the anti-prose side.

**Available truth C — Spike S1 tested one essay (Essay 016); Essay 016 is the most recently produced and structurally the most outline-amenable of the corpus.**

The research log and §6.5 CALIBRATION acknowledge this. What neither surfaces is the specificity of the concern: Essay 016's argument structure was described in Spike S1's context as already partially outline-like in its organization (the research log's R1 section notes that Essay 013 §4 "already uses an outline-like structure" for its five bulleted categories). If Essay 016 is atypically outline-amenable, the 80–85% figure is a best-case estimate, not a typical-case estimate. Earlier essays in the corpus (Essays 001–008, which predate the methodology's current argumentative discipline) may not support outline derivation at the same fidelity. This is an open question the outline does not surface — it surfaces the single-essay limitation but not the directional bias of the specific essay chosen.

Would its inclusion change the argument? Not the conclusion, but the scope-of-claim. The 80–85% should be characterized as an upper-bound estimate pending testing on earlier, less structurally-disciplined essays.

### Question 3: What would change if the dominant framing were inverted?

The outline's dominant framing: the RESEARCH artifact's essay form is the methodology's largest accidental CRESS-divergence, and the outline form is the minimally susceptible alternative supported by three convergent arguments (sycophancy-resistance, CRESS-Empirical compatibility, reduced rhetorical-vs-logical conflation).

Inverted framing: the RESEARCH essay form is one of several accumulation problems in the methodology, all sharing the same root cause (no artifact-graduation mechanism), and the appropriate response is a general graduation/compaction policy across DISCOVER, MODEL, and RESEARCH — not a form change specific to RESEARCH.

Under inversion:
- §3.3's "single largest S-Small divergence point" claim weakens: DISCOVER and MODEL's WEAK S-Small scores become equally salient, and the concentrated-in-one-phase argument loses force.
- §3.4's accidental-vs-principled table becomes the entry point, not §3.3. The accidental/principled distinction is the actual load-bearing finding, and it points toward a policy framework rather than a RESEARCH-specific form change.
- §5.7's audit-extended prose competitor and §5.2–5.8's prior-art comparanda become less decisive: if the root problem is size discipline across multiple phases, then the form within RESEARCH is a second-order question.
- The SYNTHESIZE comparison (§3.3 OBSERVATION 2) loses some of its force: SYNTHESIZE's STRONG S-Small is a design choice for a different artifact type (synthesis outline vs. research artifact), not an established precedent for the form change at RESEARCH.
- Liu et al.'s ARA evidence (§5.8) becomes more central: ARA-class infrastructure would address the accumulation problem structurally, without requiring a RESEARCH-specific form negotiation.

What would the document need to address if it took the inverted framing seriously? It would need to argue explicitly why the RESEARCH form change is the right first intervention in a multi-phase accumulation problem, rather than a graduation/compaction policy that addresses DISCOVER and MODEL simultaneously. The outline does not attempt this argument — it assumes the RESEARCH form change as the primary object and acknowledges DISCOVER and MODEL as separate concerns only in §3.4's table.

The inverted framing does not falsify the outline's conclusions: a RESEARCH form change is compatible with, and perhaps prerequisite to, a broader graduation policy. But it reveals that the outline's argument is scoped to RESEARCH-as-the-problem, not RESEARCH-as-one-of-several-related-problems.

## Outline-Form Audit Experience: Argument and Framing Audit

*Per the audit brief's special instruction — a dogfood observation on how outline form affected the argument and framing audit experience, distinct from the citation audit experience captured in the citation-audit-018-outline.md report.*

### Argument audit experience

**Gains:** The explicit CLAIM/EVIDENCE/WARRANT/CALIBRATION/SCOPE QUALIFICATION tagging system made the argument chains faster to map than in prose. In a prose essay, the auditor must first reconstruct the inferential chain from paragraph flow; here, the chain was pre-mapped by the author's tagging. This reduced the reconstruction cost for all seven argument chains. The audit could immediately locate P1 territory by checking every CLAIM bullet against its EVIDENCE/WARRANT bullets — a structural check that prose requires reading comprehension to perform. The CALIBRATION and SCOPE QUALIFICATION tags also made it straightforward to verify whether claims were appropriately hedged: the auditor checks whether a CALIBRATION bullet exists and whether its content matches the epistemic status of its parent CLAIM.

**Losses:** The visual flatness of bullets made it harder to assess whether a given CLAIM bullet's confidence level is commensurate with its CALIBRATION. Prose would signal differential confidence through hedging language ("suggests," "establishes," "demonstrates") at the sentence level; in the outline, all CLAIM bullets carry the same visual weight. The Issue 1.1 superlative ("single largest") would be harder to catch if it were expressed as a CLAIM bullet without comparing it against the §3.2 matrix — which required explicit cross-referencing of two sections. In prose, an overreaching superlative is often visible in the paragraph's rhetorical register as a claim outrunning its evidence; in outline form, the superlative sits in a CLAIM bullet with no rhetorical envelope to signal overreach.

The argument audit also required more active management of document geography: the outline's section-numbered bullet tree means that a claim and its qualification can be far apart in the document (§3.3 OBSERVATION 1 and §3.4 table, in Issue 1.1 above), and the auditor must hold both in view simultaneously. Prose's tendency to place scope qualifications within the same paragraph as the claim makes this less of an issue.

**Net assessment for argument audit:** The CLAIM/EVIDENCE/WARRANT tagging genuinely accelerates audit chain mapping and makes calibration verification more systematic. The loss is in detecting overreach at the prose-flow level — overreach that manifests as a superlative without a warrant requires cross-section comparison rather than in-paragraph reading. The argument audit is faster in outline form, but it requires explicit section-spanning cross-reference for overreach detection that prose partially surfaces through register alone.

### Framing audit experience

**Gains:** The outline's explicit SYNTHESIS bullets and META-MOVE flags made it straightforward to identify what the document chose to synthesize — and therefore what it did not choose to synthesize. In prose, a synthesis is embedded in narrative and the auditor must reconstruct it; in outline, the SYNTHESIS bullets are the record of the author's synthesis choices, making absence of alternative synthesis explicitly visible. The framing audit's Question 2 ("what truths were available but not featured") was faster to answer because the outline's claim nodes are named and enumerable: a quick cross-reference of source material claims against the outline's CLAIM bullets surfaced gaps mechanically. The framing audit's Question 3 ("what changes if the dominant framing is inverted") was more tractable because the dominant framing is stated in explicit SYNTHESIS and CLAIM bullets, not embedded in the rhetorical arc of a prose argument.

**Losses:** The framing audit's Question 1 ("what alternative framings did the evidence support") is somewhat harder in outline form because the outline collapses the evidence into its own framing's claim-node structure.
