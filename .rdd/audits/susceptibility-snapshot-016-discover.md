## Per-Signal Evaluation

### 1. Framing adoption — essay terminology in the vocabulary table

This is the primary concern raised in the dispatch brief. The specific question: did Essay 015 terminology (Einstellung, selective accessibility, scope-adaptive enforcement) enter product vocabulary in methodology voice rather than user voice?

The evidence is mixed.

**Entries with defensible user-voice framing:**

- "Einstellung" (line 449) — attributed to Luchins and Bilalic, framed as naming "the mechanism behind pattern-reuse failure." The notes field includes the academic citation and a plain-language explanation. This is a genuine user-voice entry: it names a mechanism practitioners need a word for, and the word is the one the literature uses. The Cycle 014 discover snapshot found that "State A / State B / State C" and "Architectural Isolation" were system-language entries masquerading as user-language; "Einstellung" is more defensible because practitioners who internalize this concept *will* use the word — it is not a methodology classification, it is a named cognitive phenomenon.

- "False confidence" (line 446) — "Colloquial term from testing practitioner discourse; no canonical technical equivalent." This is genuinely user-facing language. Correct register.

- "Layer-match" (line 447) — "RDD-internal term." Honest about its origin. Usable in conversation.

- "Question isolation" (line 452) — "Forming research questions before consulting the existing artifact corpus." This is the user action, described in plain terms. Defensible.

- "Expand-outward / evaluate-inward" (line 453) — explicitly attributed to "the Cycle 016 RESEARCH gate conversation." This provenance marking is correct practice and means the framing is at least grounded in observed user language, not generated by the essay.

**Entries with methodology-voice concern:**

- "Non-interrupting" (enforcement) (line 450) — "From BPMN's interrupting vs. non-interrupting boundary events." Stakeholder is "AI Agent." This is a design-architecture term, not a user-voice term. A practitioner would say "the hook doesn't block me when I'm mid-conversation" or "enforcement waits until the gate conversation is done." "Non-interrupting" is the agent's vocabulary for its own behavior design. The entry is correct information, but it sits in a user-vocabulary table where it does not fit the register.

- "Scope-adaptive" (enforcement) (line 451) — "Not a named pattern — composed from Liquibase preconditions, feature flag hierarchy, OPA conditional policy." Stakeholder is "AI Agent." This is a methodology architecture classification, not language any user would reach for. The same information could have been captured as "lighter enforcement for mini-cycles and batch cycles" — user-facing language for the same concept.

**The Cycle 014 discover snapshot's finding** was that Cycle 014's vocabulary additions drifted toward system language in six of sixteen new entries. The same pattern appears here. The two clearest instances (Non-interrupting, Scope-adaptive) are AI-Agent-stakeholder entries in the same technical register as Cycle 014's "Three-tier enforcement" and "Compound check" entries. The pattern is recurring, not new.

**Crucially, the framing adoption concern in the dispatch brief (Essay 015 → product vocabulary) is partially but not fully warranted.** The essay terms that entered the vocabulary table are mostly attributed (Einstellung has literature attribution, non-interrupting has BPMN attribution, scope-adaptive notes it is not a named pattern). The concern is not attribution — it is register: these are essay-analytic terms that entered a user-facing vocabulary table without being tested against whether practitioners would reach for these words in conversation.

### 2. Value tensions — binary collapse from spectra

The dispatch brief asks whether value tensions that surfaced as spectra collapsed into binary framings without the alternatives being examined.

The four new value tensions:

1. **Scenario-level coverage vs. criterion-level coverage** — presented as two poles. The spectrum between them (partial layer-match verification, risk-stratified verification) is not examined. The tension preserves the unresolved-ness ("When is the overhead warranted — for every criterion, or only for criteria that specify integration-level behavior?") without engaging what lies between the poles. This is an acceptable tension framing for product discovery, where the goal is to name the tension, not resolve it.

2. **ADR immutability vs. navigational simplicity** — same structure. Two poles; the spectrum is not examined. The tension names the real choice without exploring hybrid approaches (e.g., supersession chains with navigation-aiding current-state summaries). Again, acceptable for product discovery.

3. **Scope-adaptive enforcement vs. structural guarantee preservation** — the most interesting case. The tension names the "design judgment from the research: compose existing enforcement primitives rather than building a separate enforcement tier." This is a resolution stated *within* the tension entry. The tension entry reads as: "this is the tension, and here is the answer." That structure collapses the tension at the moment of naming it. The product discovery document's value tensions are supposed to hold unresolved-ness; this one resolves the design question and then names it as a tension. The collapse is subtle — the phrase "The tension remains in calibration" preserves some unresolved-ness — but the design judgment is stated declaratively, not held open.

4. **Structural encoding vs. cognitive mechanism as first line** — presents two options and states the resolution ("the first line of defense should be structurally verifiable"). Same pattern as Tension 3: the resolution is embedded in the tension statement. The methodology's Invariant 8 framing is stated as the answer before the tension is held open. A reader of this entry receives the methodology's conclusion, not an open tension.

**The spectrum-to-binary assessment:** All four tensions are binary in structure. Two (Tensions 1 and 2) hold the binary open appropriately. Two (Tensions 3 and 4) resolve the binary within the tension entry by stating the essay's design judgment as the answer. This is a mild narrowing signal — the product discovery vocabulary is inheriting the essay's conclusions rather than re-examining them from a user-value perspective.

## Pattern Interpretation

The Cycle 014 discover snapshot documented a compounding propagation pattern: research-phase user crystallizations entered the discover artifact as settled vocabulary without attribution. The recommendation was a 15-minute provenance pass before MODEL.

**This discover phase shows a related but distinct pattern.**

The Cycle 014 pattern was: user-originated crystallizations entered the vocabulary table as if independently generated. The concern was provenance loss.

The Cycle 016 pattern is: essay-originated framings entered the vocabulary table in methodology voice rather than user voice. The concern is register mismatch — not that the framings are wrong or unattributed, but that "Non-interrupting" and "Scope-adaptive" are design-architecture terms that a practitioner would not reach for in conversation, even though they are correctly attributed to BPMN and composition-of-primitives respectively.

These are distinguishable concerns:
- Cycle 014: *who* originated the framing (user vs. agent) was lost
- Cycle 016: *whose voice* the framing speaks in (user vs. methodology designer) is mismatched

**Is this earned confidence or sycophantic reinforcement?**

The essay engagement was substantively deep (documented research on five tracks, literature-grounded, hypothesis-tested). The user's RESEARCH gate engagement was genuinely challenging. These are earned-confidence signals for the essay's conclusions.

The product discovery update's concern is not that the essay's conclusions are wrong — it is that the translation from essay-voice conclusions to user-voice vocabulary entries is incomplete for two specific entries, and that two of the four new value tensions embed their resolution within the tension statement rather than holding the design question open.

This pattern is consistent with **competent but register-inattentive update work** rather than with sycophantic reinforcement. The framing adoption signal is present but mild. The agent adopted essay terminology without testing it against whether practitioners would use that language — this is the same pattern the Cycle 014 discover snapshot identified, but milder in this cycle because the Cycle 016 entries include provenance attribution that the Cycle 014 entries lacked.

The batch format does not appear to have driven mechanical processing. The seven product debt entries and five inversions show differentiated, substantive engagement. The batch pressure concern is not confirmed.

The two embedded-resolution tensions (Tensions 3 and 4 in the value tensions section) are the clearest signal. These tensions inherit the essay's design judgments without translating them into open product questions. A product discovery artifact that holds these open would read: "Smaller-scoped cycles need enforcement that can recognize its own inapplicability — the question is whether composing existing primitives is the right level of intervention or whether a dedicated enforcement tier is needed." The current artifact states the design judgment (compose existing primitives) as the answer.

## Recommendation

**Grounding Reframe recommended — scoped to register and resolution-embedding, not to the cycle's research conclusions.**

**What is uncertain:**

Two vocabulary entries are in methodology-architecture voice rather than user voice:
- "Non-interrupting" (enforcement) — AI Agent stakeholder — named after a BPMN architectural pattern rather than the user experience ("enforcement waits until gate conversations are done")
- "Scope-adaptive" (enforcement) — AI Agent stakeholder — a composition-of-primitives classification rather than a user-facing behavior description ("lighter enforcement that checks its own applicability before firing")

Two value tensions state the research essay's design judgment as the resolution within the tension entry:
- "Scope-adaptive enforcement vs. structural guarantee preservation" — the tension states "design judgment from the research: compose existing enforcement primitives rather than building a separate enforcement tier" as a resolved position
- "Structural encoding vs. cognitive mechanism as first line" — the tension states the Invariant 8 resolution ("first line of defense should be structurally verifiable") as settled within the entry

**What the user would be building on without grounding:** A vocabulary table with two AI-Agent-stakeholder entries in system-architecture register that MODEL and DECIDE will inherit as settled vocabulary. A value tensions section with two entries that appear to hold design questions open but actually state the essay's answers — meaning downstream phases will read these as validated product-level decisions rather than as research-phase design judgments awaiting DECIDE-level scrutiny.

**Concrete grounding action — lightweight, before MODEL:**

1. For the two vocabulary entries ("Non-interrupting," "Scope-adaptive"): the user decides whether these belong in the vocabulary table as user-facing terms or in the domain model glossary as methodology-design terms. If kept in the vocabulary table, a brief reframe toward user-behavior language would improve the register: "enforcement that waits for active gate conversations to finish" and "enforcement that checks whether it applies before firing" are user-voice equivalents.

2. For the two embedded-resolution tensions ("Scope-adaptive enforcement vs. structural guarantee preservation," "Structural encoding vs. cognitive mechanism as first line"): consider whether the design judgment stated within each tension is a user-level decision (should live in the product discovery) or a methodology-architecture decision (should be carried to DECIDE as an open question with the research's recommendation, not as a settled product-level tension). If the latter, reformulate the tension to hold the design question open rather than encoding the research recommendation as the product answer.

This is not a revision of the research conclusions — those are well-grounded. It is a translation pass: the same findings, in user-voice rather than essay-voice, with the design questions held open for DECIDE rather than resolved in product discovery.

**Why this is not a blocking concern for phase advancement:** The research findings underlying these entries are well-grounded, the inversions are substantively engaged, the product debt entries are differentiated, and the user demonstrated active engagement with accuracy (catching stale entries). The register and resolution-embedding concerns are worth surfacing before MODEL builds its domain vocabulary on top of this vocabulary table, but they are a 15-minute translation pass, not a cycle-level revision.

**Trajectory note for MODEL:** The Cycle 014 discover snapshot identified vocabulary drift toward system language as a recurring pattern. This cycle exhibits the same pattern in the same AI-Agent-stakeholder entries. The conformance audit's future attention to the vocabulary table's register would address this systematically; for now, the MODEL phase should treat the two system-architecture entries as candidates requiring register examination before lifting them into the domain glossary.
