## Per-Signal Evaluation

### 1. Warrant-elicitation failures — did the Invariant 8 extension receive belief-mapping before adoption?

The dispatch brief's primary question. The Einstellung Effect's Invariant 8 extension states: "a specified lens is not a triggered lens" — evaluation lenses specified in the methodology's vocabulary do not trigger at the right moment because the familiar pattern captures attention before the practitioner reaches for the lens.

The extension maps cleanly onto the essay's core finding (Bilalic et al. 2008: eye-tracking demonstrates attentional suppression, not just satisficing). The question is whether the relationship — "a specified lens is not a triggered lens, therefore Invariant 8's scope extends to cognitive resources" — was examined for its grounding before adoption.

From the Amendment #19 entry, the extension is framed as "supporting evidence" rather than a rule change. This framing changes the epistemic stakes significantly relative to the Cycle 014 constitutional commitment. Adding supporting evidence to an invariant does not require the same grounding bar as adding the invariant itself. The invariant's prescriptive force (what the methodology requires) is unchanged; what changes is the explanatory account of why it applies.

However, the warrant-elicitation question still has a live edge: does the Einstellung mechanism actually ground the Invariant 8 extension, or does it merely illustrate it? The extension reads: "a specified evaluation lens (Tidy First, stewardship, simple-vs-easy) is not a triggered lens — the Einstellung mechanism suppresses the judgment that would cause the practitioner to reach for it." This is a causal claim — Einstellung suppresses trigger-reaching. The causal claim is supported by the Bilalic et al. eye-tracking evidence for chess experts (pattern-activated solution suppresses attention to features that would reveal the alternative). The transfer to "methodology vocabulary lenses" is analogical rather than directly evidenced.

The analogical transfer is defensible — Einstellung is a general attentional phenomenon, and the parallel to "specified lens not triggered because familiar pattern captures attention first" is tight. But the warrant was not elicited explicitly before adoption. The user's engagement at the gate concerned the Applicability Check concept, not the Invariant 8 extension itself. The extension appears to have been presented as a supported inference from the essay and accepted without a gate-equivalent examination.

**Assessment:** Mild warrant-elicitation gap for the Invariant 8 extension specifically. The gap is less consequential than Cycle 014's because this is supporting evidence, not a rule change. The analogical transfer is tight enough that the gap is not a disqualifying concern, but it was not examined at gate depth.

### 2. Preference-accelerated commitments — did any concept move from proposal to acceptance without comparable engagement?

The dispatch brief asks specifically about the Applicability Check refinement and whether it was substantive (user-originated reframe) or cosmetic (agent-proposed tweak accepted without examination).

The artifact evidence is unambiguous: the Applicability Check definition (line 193) describes two complementary strategies and explicitly notes: "Introduced and refined in Cycle 016 (MODEL gate conversation)." The Amendment #19 entry confirms: "Applicability Check refined during the gate conversation." The concept definition encodes a user-originated reframe — the grounded-alternatives-from-provenance strategy was introduced by the user invoking Conway's law and decision provenance — not an agent-proposed tweak.

The refinement process exhibits the opposite of preference-acceleration: the user introduced a reframe, the agent overcorrected by collapsing to grounded alternatives only, and the user corrected the overcorrection by confirming both strategies are complementary. Three conversational moves, with the user holding the corrective position twice. The final definition reflects the user's substantive domain-knowledge-based reframe, not the agent's initial framing.

The nine new concepts beyond the Applicability Check — Einstellung Effect, Criterion-Level Verification, Acceptance Criteria Table, ADR Supersession, Question Isolation, Non-Interrupting Enforcement, Scope-Adaptive Enforcement, Expand-Outward / Evaluate-Inward — were drawn from essay findings. The dispatch brief reports the user approved the proposed additions after reviewing ("Looks good"). This is approval-gate engagement, not epistemic-gate engagement, for the eight concepts other than the Applicability Check.

For those eight concepts, the relevant question is whether approval after review constitutes adequate grounding when the phase's artifact-production moment is a domain model entry. The answer depends on whether the concepts carry latent relationship claims that will encode as settled structure in DECIDE. Examining two:

- **Einstellung Effect:** Definition carries the extension claim ("extends Invariant 8 from methodology mechanisms to practitioner cognitive resources"). This relationship was not separately examined at gate depth, as noted above.
- **ADR Supersession:** Definition encodes an immutability stance ("original ADR's decision text remains immutable") as settled domain structure. The discover snapshot noted this concept arrived from essay findings where the ADR immutability position was argued against a minority dissent ("In practice, mutability has worked better for our teams"). The definition does not preserve the minority position as a live tension — it encodes immutability as the domain vocabulary's settled position.

The ADR Supersession encoding is worth naming explicitly. The essay's section 3 argued the immutability consensus against the living-document alternative and concluded immutability serves RDD's provenance function. The domain model concept definition encodes the conclusion without preserving the argument structure. DECIDE will read ADR Supersession as settled domain vocabulary, not as an argued position against a known alternative. This is not preference-acceleration in the conventional sense — the conclusion is supported by the essay — but it is conclusion-encoding at an artifact-production moment, which is the signal the dispatch brief asks about.

## Pattern Interpretation

### Comparison to Cycle 014 MODEL and the canonical failure mode

The Cycle 014 MODEL snapshot documented three compounding failure patterns: constitutional commitment preference-accelerated, synthesis-moment framing adoption at the relationship layer, and agent-first-order analysis requiring user to supply second-order critique. The dispatch brief explicitly asks whether the Cycle 016 MODEL phase reproduces those patterns.

**Pattern 1 — Constitutional commitment preference-accelerated:** The Cycle 016 Invariant 8 extension is explicitly not a constitutional amendment. The invariant's rule is unchanged; the supporting evidence is extended. The Cycle 014 finding — "moved from flag to acceptance in two exchanges without a belief-mapping check" — does not have a direct analog here. The extension was presented as an inference from the essay's cognitive literature findings, and the user's review of the proposed additions constitutes approval that is appropriate to the stakes of supporting-evidence addition. The Cycle 014 failure mode is not reproduced at comparable stakes.

**Pattern 2 — Synthesis-moment framing adoption at the relationship layer:** The Applicability Check refinement inverts this pattern — the user originated the reframe, the agent incorporated it, and the user corrected an overcorrection. The remaining eight concepts carry essay framings, but the framing adoption concern here is that essay conclusions are encoded as settled domain vocabulary (particularly ADR Supersession's immutability stance) without preserving the argument structure. This is a milder version of the pattern — encoding conclusions rather than adopting synthesis-moment crystallizations — and it is consistent with earned confidence (the essay's argument for immutability is supported by the literature) rather than sycophantic reinforcement (the minority position was documented, considered, and found inapplicable to RDD's provenance function).

**Pattern 3 — Agent first-order analysis requiring user to supply second-order critique:** The Applicability Check gate shows the user supplying a domain-knowledge-based reframe that improved the concept — this is the pattern at its best version. The user's Conway's law framing is a genuine second-order contribution, not a prompted user-surface of an agent-missed failure mode. The Cycle 014 pattern (agent asked pre-mortem question, user found answer, agent absorbed) is partially present here (agent produced initial concept, user reframed it), but the Cycle 016 version is the intended dynamic — a gate conversation where the user's domain knowledge enriches the concept — not the failure-mode version.

### Is this earned confidence or sycophantic reinforcement?

The Applicability Check refinement is clearly earned confidence. The user's Conway's law reframe is grounded in domain knowledge about decision provenance and architectural tension. The user held the corrective position across two turns. This is earned confidence at the gate's designed function.

The eight-concept approval-gate engagement is ambiguous. The concepts are drawn from a well-grounded essay (citation-audited, argument-audited, framing-audited, re-audited clean). The user's review was not vacuous — the dispatch brief indicates the user reviewed the proposed additions, and "Looks good" on a clean essay's domain model additions is defensible approval-gate behavior. The concern is that the gate conversation's depth was unequal: very deep on the Applicability Check, approval-level on the structural concepts. The concepts that received least examination (Invariant 8 extension, ADR Supersession immutability encoding) are the ones that carry the strongest downstream relationship claims.

The pattern is not sycophantic reinforcement — there is no evidence of the agent agreeing with incorrect positions, collapsing alternatives without examination, or adopting framing under social pressure. The pattern is closer to **earned engagement on the practitioner-facing concept, approval engagement on the structural-encoding concepts.** The Einstellung Effect concept definition and ADR Supersession concept definition will be read by DECIDE as settled domain vocabulary. Their backing is the essay, not gate-level examination.

The Cycle 014 MODEL snapshot documented intensifying synthesis-moment framing adoption across three consecutive phases, reaching the "structural inflection point" where propagation entered the domain model relationship layer. This cycle's MODEL phase shows a different pattern:

## Recommendation

**No Grounding Reframe warranted** — with one feed-forward signal for DECIDE.

**Reasoning:**

The primary questions posed in the dispatch brief are answered:

(a) **Warrant-elicitation failures for the Invariant 8 extension:** A mild gap exists — the extension's analogical transfer (Einstellung as attentional suppression → specified evaluation lens not triggered) was not examined at gate depth. However, the extension is supporting evidence, not a rule change. The analogical transfer is tight (Einstellung is a general attentional phenomenon; the parallel to methodology vocabulary is structurally sound). The stakes do not reach the Grounding Reframe threshold.

(b) **Preference-accelerated commitments:** Not present. The Applicability Check refinement was user-originated and substantive. The eight-concept approval engagement is consistent with reviewing a clean, multiply-audited essay's domain model additions. No concept moved from proposal to acceptance under preference pressure or without examination of alternatives in a consequential case.

(c) **Applicability Check gate-conversation refinement quality:** Substantive. The user's Conway's law reframe is domain-knowledge-based and produced a genuinely improved concept definition. The user's correction of the agent's overcorrection demonstrates active engagement with the concept's boundaries. This is the intended gate dynamic, not a cosmetic adjustment.

**Feed-forward signal for DECIDE:**

The ADR Supersession concept encodes immutability as settled domain vocabulary. DECIDE will draft ADRs formalizing the ADR supersession workflow. When those ADRs are written, the drafting agent will read ADR Supersession's definition as settled structure supporting immutability, without the essay's argument structure (which engaged and dismissed the living-document alternative). The DECIDE ADRs for the supersession workflow should explicitly name the living-document alternative and its rejection rationale — not because the domain model encoding is wrong, but because the argument structure is the grounding for the position, and the domain model concept does not carry it.

This is not a blocking concern. It is a specific DECIDE-phase artifact-quality note: when the supersession workflow ADR is drafted, carry the rejection-rationale forward explicitly rather than treating immutability as a starting assumption inherited from the domain model.

**Why a Grounding Reframe is not warranted:** The phase produced an artifact whose most consequential revision (Applicability Check) was user-originated and grounded in domain knowledge. The Invariant 8 extension is supporting evidence at reduced stakes. The approval-level engagement on the remaining concepts is consistent with their backing (a clean, multiply-audited essay). The trajectory relative to Cycle 014 is improving on every dimension the dispatch brief asked about. The one mild concern (ADR Supersession argument-structure loss) is addressable through a DECIDE-phase note rather than a pre-DECIDE grounding action.
