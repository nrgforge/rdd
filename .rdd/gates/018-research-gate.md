## Belief-mapping question composed for this gate

The functional belief-mapping question at this gate was the Target 1 Grounding Reframe action from the susceptibility snapshot:

> "Given the full evidence produced by the cycle — Loop C's findings, the Cochrane hybrid's 'gold standard' verdict in the lit-review, the Spike S1 80–85% preservation finding, the citation comparison — is there a different artifact form (the Cochrane hybrid's layered-audit-with-structure approach; the structured-abstract-plus-synthesis hybrid; audit-extended prose with mandated explicit calibration bullets) that would satisfy the susceptibility-surface-minimization goal as well as outline form does? If the answer is 'yes, but outline is simpler and available now,' that is a decision DECIDE should encode explicitly as the rationale, not inherit as a given. If the answer is 'no, the evidence distinguishes outline form from these alternatives,' then DECIDE's ADR can cite the specific evidence that rules them out."

This is the form-question's foundational belief test — the question whose answer determines whether outline form was derived from evidence or absorbed as the entry hypothesis.

## User's response

The user requested the grounding action be pursued directly. The agent surfaced a head-to-head comparison of three alternatives (Cochrane hybrid; structured-abstract-plus-synthesis hybrid; audit-extended prose with mandated CALIBRATION bullets) against outline-with-inline-citations on the three criteria (audit-fidelity, susceptibility-surface-minimization, downstream-feed-forward preservation) plus an RDD-context fit dimension.

The composite verdict the user accepted:

- **Cochrane hybrid:** outline NOT distinguished by criteria; outline preferred over Cochrane only on availability/cycle-rhythm grounds. The Cochrane-scale-incompatibility constraint is the load-bearing rationale, not the criterion comparison.
- **Structured-abstract-plus-synthesis hybrid:** outline NOT distinguished by criteria; structurally similar to outline form, choice is a gradient question (prose connective tissue vs bullet structure). Outline is functionally a sub-case.
- **Audit-extended prose with mandated CALIBRATION bullets:** outline GENUINELY DISTINGUISHED by Loop C's critical-flaw argument ("audit-extended prose cannot separate rhetorical force from logical force, which means framing adoption can occur within a well-headed section without detection"). Adding discipline bullets within prose does not address the structural flaw.

The user's selected response: "Yes — record this framing, proceed. The honest characterization above is the grounded answer."

The grounded framing that DECIDE inherits: outline-with-inline-citations is **the available instantiation of the structured-abstract-plus-synthesis family at RDD's cycle-rhythm scale**, distinguished from audit-extended prose by the Loop C critical-flaw evidence, and preferred over Cochrane on scale-compatibility grounds. NOT "outline is uniquely best by criteria comparison."
